## 1. THE STRATEGIC SITUATION

For 2024, the Mercedes-Benz Group reported revenue of EUR 145.6 billion (down 4.5% YoY), EBIT of EUR 13.6 billion (down 30.8% YoY), and net profit of EUR 10.4 billion (down 28.4% YoY). This financial deterioration stems from three converging crises:

- **The EV Demand Gap:** In 2024, Mercedes-Benz Cars recorded 185,100 battery-electric vehicles (BEVs), a 23% decline from the prior year.
- **The China Crisis:** Mercedes-Benz Group AG's car sales fell due to a deepening slump in China, where once-dominant Western brands are losing ground to new domestic challengers pushing into the luxury segment.
- **The Margin Collapse:** The company's 2024 operating results showed a 31% decline to 13.6 billion euros, with passenger car segment margins falling from 12.6% to 8.1%.

## 2. EXTERNAL ANALYSIS

### PESTEL Analysis

**Political:** In 2024, the EU imposed provisional tariffs on Chinese EVs. Political stability significantly impacts Mercedes-Benz's operations, and consumer preferences are shifting toward electric and hybrid vehicles.

**Economic:** Mercedes attributed the decline in sales of its top-of-the-range models to weaker demand for electric cars, model changes and market conditions in China. The crisis in the Chinese real estate market has put many wealthy customers off buying.

**Technological:** Mercedes-Benz has announced its own proprietary MB.OS operating system, developed in-house and scheduled for launch in 2024. It will allow Mercedes-Benz to centralize the control of all the vehicles' domains and also its consumer interfaces. Furthermore, Mercedes-Benz and NVIDIA plan to enter into a cooperation to create a revolutionary in-vehicle computing system and AI computing infrastructure, to be rolled out across next-generation Mercedes-Benz vehicles from 2024.

**Environmental:** Mercedes-Benz has identified 24 raw materials with increased human rights and environmental risks. In 2024, it assessed 65% of these materials.

---

### Porter's Five Forces

**Competitive Rivalry (HIGH):** In 2024, the company faces pressure from established rivals like BMW and Audi, as well as new entrants like Tesla and various Chinese EV manufacturers.

**Buyer Power (HIGH):** The company has been forced to offer consumer incentives to combat slowing EV demand, particularly in China where luxury spending has been hit by economic slowdown.

**Threat of New Entrants (MODERATE):** The entry barriers of this industry are very high, including brand equity, huge initial investments, extensive marketing costs, strict government regulations, and economies of scale — all of which make the threat of new entrants relatively low. However, Chinese tech-company entrants represent a growing medium-term concern.

## 3. INTERNAL ANALYSIS

### SWOT

**Strengths:** Mercedes-Benz's brand is a significant competitive advantage, built on a legacy of quality and luxury. This strong brand equity fosters customer loyalty, allowing the company to maintain premium pricing.

**Weaknesses:** By 2024, the company was pushing back sales targets for electrified vehicles, targeting 50 percent electrified sales by 2030, rather than 2025 — multiple revisions that have damaged investor confidence.

**Opportunities:** By 2025, Mercedes-Benz intends to target €1 billion from software-enabled, features-on-demand.

**Threats:** Mercedes-Benz has lost market share in the world's largest car market, alongside rivals BMW and Audi, as local brands including BYD, NIO and Li Auto gain ground with technology-focused premium vehicles offered at lower prices.

### Value Chain Analysis

Mercedes-Benz Cars invested €8.7 billion in R&D during 2024. On the operations side, production costs will decrease by ten percent between 2024 and 2027, achieved through greater efficiency in production and logistics thanks to digitalisation, as well as the use of artificial intelligence. In supply chain terms, a 10% reduction in material costs is targeted through deeper localisation of parts in the China supply chain.

## 6. CONCLUSION

Mercedes-Benz possesses the brand equity, engineering heritage, and financial resilience to navigate the electric crossroads — but incremental cost-cutting alone is insufficient. Mercedes-Benz has outlined aggressive cost-reduction targets, including a 10% cut in production costs by 2027 through material optimization, fixed-cost reductions, and enhanced global production flexibility. However, the deeper strategic requirement is a coherent repositioning: protecting Cash Cows, converting EV Question Marks into Stars, and building a defensible software and ultra-luxury moat that no Chinese or American rival can easily replicate. Mercedes-Benz is going all-in on electrification, planning to launch 18 new models in 2026 — many of them fully electric — in what it calls the biggest product rollout in its history. With disciplined execution of the six recommendations above, the three-pointed star can emerge from this period of disruption as a stronger, more resilient, and more digitally sophisticated luxury leader.
