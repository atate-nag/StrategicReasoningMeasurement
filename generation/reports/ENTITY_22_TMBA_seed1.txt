# 📋 STRATEGIC CASE STUDY: ASTRAZENECA PLC
## *"Navigating the Patent Cliff, Geopolitical Risk, and the Road to $80 Billion"*

---

## 🏢 COMPANY BACKGROUND
AstraZeneca revealed its bold ambition to deliver **$80 billion in Total Revenue by 2030**, up from $45.8 billion in 2023, to be achieved through significant growth in its oncology, biopharmaceuticals, and rare disease portfolio, and by launching an expected **20 new medicines** before the end of the decade. This ambition is the lens through which the entire case study is framed.

---

## 🌍 PART 2: PESTEL ANALYSIS

### Political
AstraZeneca has appealed the IRA's drug price negotiation provisions, joining fellow Big Pharma players Johnson & Johnson and Bristol Myers Squibb, which have also appealed similar losses. On the China front, AstraZeneca prepaid approximately **$3.5 million in import taxes** to Chinese customs authorities to address part of the investigation, though it cautioned that it could still face additional fines of up to **$17.5 million**.

### Economic
The biopharma industry has experienced a tumultuous period, with the loss of exclusivity for biologics — which began in earnest in 2023 — putting an estimated **$300 billion in revenue at risk** between 2023 and 2028. For AstraZeneca specifically, FARXIGA, the company's top-performing cardiovascular drug generating approximately **$7.7 billion in revenue in 2024**, faces patent expiry in **2026**.

### Technological
Daiichi Sankyo's pipeline includes promising candidates like datopotamab deruxtecan and patritumab deruxtecan, with its proprietary **DXd payload technology** enabling higher potency and tumour-specific activity. AstraZeneca's collaboration with Daiichi Sankyo on Enhertu is now annualising at a **$5 billion run rate**.

---

## ⚔️ PART 3: PORTER'S FIVE FORCES

| Force | Intensity | Key Driver |
|---|---|---|
| Competitive Rivalry | **HIGH** | ADC crowding; GLP-1 disruption; Chinese biopharma |
| Threat of New Entrants | **LOW–MEDIUM** | Chinese biotech; AI-enabled discovery |
| Buyer Power | **HIGH** | IRA; China VBP; European HTA |
| Supplier Power | **MEDIUM** | Biologics CMO concentration |
| Threat of Substitutes | **MEDIUM** | Biosimilars; GLP-1s; cell therapy |

AstraZeneca has maintained its competitive position through its **robust pipeline of innovative drugs, strategic partnerships, and focus on personalised medicine**. However, in the pharmaceutical industry, the **bargaining power of buyers is high**, as governments and insurance companies can negotiate lower prices for drugs due to their significant purchasing power.

---

## 🔬 PART 4: RESOURCE-BASED VIEW (VRIO)

The case applies Barney's VRIO framework to identify AstraZeneca's most durable competitive advantages:

- ✅ **ADC Platform (Enhertu/Dato-DXd)** → **Sustained Competitive Advantage** (Valuable, Rare, Inimitable, Organised)
- ✅ **Oncology Pipeline Depth** → **Sustained Advantage** — AstraZeneca announced an unprecedented **16 positive Phase III trials** in 2024
- ⚠️ **Tagrisso** → *Temporary Advantage* — Lazertinib (BeiGene), a third-generation EGFR TKI, demonstrated comparable efficacy and may erode market share
- ❌ **Farxiga** → *Imminent Disadvantage* due to 2026 LOE

---

## 📉 PART 5: THE PATENT CLIFF PROBLEM

Several high-value AstraZeneca assets are approaching the end of their patent protection by 2030, with Farxiga's patent set to expire in 2026, placing mounting pressure on the company to offset the anticipated revenue loss through pipeline innovation or strategic lifecycle management.

Enhertu will retain crucial patent protections past 2030, according to William Blair, while AstraZeneca has done a "commendable job" of building out its product portfolio, particularly in the oncology space.
