# 📋 ABBVIE INC.: NAVIGATING THE POST-HUMIRA ERA — Case Study Overview

---

## 🏢 The Strategic Situation

AbbVie's U.S. Humira net revenues fell 54.5% year-on-year, with international sales also declining following the loss of exclusivity in January 2023. The first U.S. Humira biosimilar, Amgen's Amjevita, launched in January 2023, kicking off the competitive phase that AbbVie had spent years preparing for. Despite this, the company reported a full-year net revenue increase of 3.7% to $56.334 billion and delivered significant growth in its immunology, oncology, and neuroscience portfolios.

---

## 🔬 Framework 1: Porter's Five Forces

The analysis reveals a **moderately attractive but intensifying** competitive environment:

- **Competitive Rivalry (HIGH):** Imbruvica saw its sales drop from $8.7 billion (2021) to $5.9 billion by 2023, eroded by next-generation BTK inhibitors like AstraZeneca's Calquence and BeiGene's Brukinsa.
- **Buyer Power (HIGH):** The Inflation Reduction Act introduced Medicare drug price negotiation, and PBMs actively preferred lower-cost biosimilars on formularies.
- **Supplier Power (MODERATE):** Suppliers of specialized raw materials and active pharmaceutical ingredients hold significant power over AbbVie due to the complex nature of these components and the high costs associated with switching providers.

---

## 💎 Framework 2: Resource-Based View (VRIO)

AbbVie's most powerful resource is its immunology platform. Through aggressive R&D investment, portfolio diversification, and the rapid commercialisation of Skyrizi and Rinvoq, the company has not only offset Humira's decline but positioned itself as a high-growth biopharma leader. Skyrizi and Rinvoq generated a combined $25.8 billion in 2025.

---

## 📊 Framework 3: SWOT Analysis

**Key Strength:** AbbVie reaffirms expectations for a high single-digit compound annual revenue growth rate through 2029 and raises the 2027 combined sales outlook for Skyrizi and Rinvoq to more than $31 billion.

**Key Weakness/Threat (M&A Risk):** AbbVie's two Phase 2 EMPOWER trials investigating emraclidine as a once-daily, oral monotherapy treatment for adults with schizophrenia did not meet their primary endpoint — just months after the $8.7 billion Cerevel acquisition closed.

**Key Opportunity:** ImmunoGen's pipeline complements AbbVie's existing oncology pipeline with potential to be transformative across multiple solid tumours and hematologic malignancies.

---

## 🗺️ Framework 4: Ansoff Growth Matrix

AbbVie is simultaneously pursuing all four quadrants:
- **Market Penetration:** Skyrizi growing >50% in existing markets
- **Market Development:** International expansion of immunology and aesthetics
- **Product Development:** ADC pipeline, indication expansions — the core strategic priority
- **Diversification:** The Allergan acquisition significantly expands and diversifies AbbVie's revenue base and complements existing leadership positions in Immunology, with new growth opportunities in Neuroscience and a global aesthetics business with leading brands including Botox and Juvederm.
