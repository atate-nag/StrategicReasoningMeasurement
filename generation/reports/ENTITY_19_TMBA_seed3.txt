# 🥃 Strategic Case Study: Diageo plc — Navigating a Multi-Front Strategic Crisis

## *Premiumisation Under Pressure: Tariffs, Moderation, and the Fight for the Next Generation of Drinkers*

---

## 1. Introduction & Company Overview

Diageo plc is the world's largest producer and marketer of premium spirits. Diageo is a global leader in beverage alcohol with an outstanding collection of brands including Johnnie Walker, Crown Royal, J&B and Buchanan's whiskies, Smirnoff, Cîroc and Ketel One vodkas, Captain Morgan, Baileys, Don Julio, Tanqueray and Guinness. Diageo is a global company, and its products are sold in nearly 180 countries around the world, with the company listed on both the London Stock Exchange (DGE) and the New York Stock Exchange (DEO).

Diageo's strategic consolidation of renowned brands has solidified its position as the world's largest producer and marketer of premium spirits, with a journey that began with a clear vision to dominate the premium spirits market. Diageo's competitive edge is built on a robust portfolio of 13 billion-dollar brands, an extensive global reach, and a well-established distribution network.

Despite this formidable position, Diageo finds itself at a genuine strategic crossroads. The Diageo SWOT analysis reveals a powerful incumbent at a strategic crossroads — its formidable strengths, including an iconic brand portfolio and unparalleled global distribution, provide a stable foundation. However, recent performance, particularly the inventory crisis in Latin America and volume softness, has exposed vulnerabilities. This case study analyses the strategic situation facing Diageo, applies relevant academic frameworks, and proposes a coherent action plan.

---

## 2. The Strategic Situation: Defining the Problem

Diageo's strategic challenges can be grouped into four interconnected themes:

### 2.1 Revenue Decline and Regional Imbalance

Organic net sales declined $129 million or 0.6% in FY2024, with positive price/mix of 2.9 percentage points more than offset by a 3.5% volume decline, primarily driven by a 21.1% decline in the Latin America and Caribbean (LAC) region. Organic operating profit declined by $304 million or 4.8%.

Reported net sales of $11.0 billion declined 1.4%, due to a $167 million unfavourable foreign exchange impact and an organic net sales decline driven by a $310 million or 23% decline in Latin America and Caribbean. Excluding the impact of LAC, reported net sales grew $72 million or 0.7%, and organic net sales grew $243 million or 2.5%.

### 2.2 US Market Headwinds and Tariff Exposure

In FY2025, solid organic net sales growth in Europe, LAC and Africa was offset by weakness in Chinese white spirits impacting Asia Pacific results and softer performance in North America, as US Spirits declined reflecting weak consumer confidence.

Diageo faces an approximate US$150 million unmitigated annual impact on its business from the 10% tariff placed by US President Donald Trump on both UK and European imports to the United States. Diageo removed its medium-term guidance due to macroeconomic and geopolitical uncertainty, with US tariffs potentially resulting in a $200 million hit to second-half operating profit — primarily from disrupted tequila sales.

### 2.3 The Moderation Mega-Trend

Nearly half of Americans are trying to drink less alcohol in 2025, a number markedly higher among younger generations. In just the last two years, the level of interest in sober-curious lifestyles has increased 44%, and fully a quarter of American adults report drinking no alcohol in 2024.

The rise of alcohol-free and low-alcohol beverages — collectively known as NoLo products — reflects the evolving "sober-curious" movement. A growing segment of consumers is prioritising health and mindfulness over habitual or socially motivated drinking.

### 2.4 Intensifying Competition

Walmart, Costco, and Tesco control 59.2% of global alcohol retail distribution channels. These retailers negotiate prices aggressively, demanding volume discounts. At the same time, craft distillers and agile challenger brands are eroding Diageo's share in premium categories such as tequila and whiskey.

## 3. External Analysis: PESTEL Framework

The PESTEL framework (Johnson et al., 2017) maps the macro-environmental forces shaping Diageo's strategic context.

| Dimension | Key Factors |
|---|---|
| **Political** | US tariffs; trade war risk; regulatory divergence across 180 markets |
| **Economic** | Weak consumer confidence; FX headwinds; inflationary cost pressures |
| **Sociocultural** | Sober-curious movement; health & wellness; Gen Z moderation |
| **Technological** | E-commerce growth; data analytics; digital marketing |
| **Environmental** | Sustainability demands; water stewardship; grain-to-glass accountability |
| **Legal** | Advertising restrictions; age verification laws; excise duty regimes |

**Political:** Diageo has anticipated and planned for a number of potential scenarios regarding tariffs in recent months. The confirmation of the implementation of tariffs in the US, whilst anticipated, creates real headwinds for the business.

**Economic:** While consumers continue to be cautious in this environment, Diageo is focused on strengthening the resilience of its business through operational excellence, productivity and strategic investments to win quality market share.

**Sociocultural:** One key driver behind the decline in alcohol consumption among younger generations is the broader cultural shift toward wellness and moderation. Consumers today are more focused on their physical and mental well-being, and alcohol is increasingly seen as counterproductive to these goals. The rise of the "sober curious" movement and trends such as Dry January highlight how younger consumers are re-evaluating their relationship with alcohol.

**Technological:** Diageo's sales and marketing strategy centres on premiumisation, omnichannel distribution, and analytics-led consumer engagement; campaigns in 2024–2025 emphasise cultural relevance, sustainability and digital-first experiences to drive luxury positioning and market share growth.

**Economic (Industry Long-Term):** Total Beverage Alcohol (TBA) is resilient and growing, with the spirits category growing even faster. TBA has grown at 4.4% CAGR in the 10 years through to 2023, and international spirits has grown at 5.1% over the same period.

## 4. Industry Analysis: Porter's Five Forces

Porter's Five Forces (Porter, 1980) provides a structural analysis of the premium spirits industry's attractiveness and Diageo's competitive position within it.

### Force 1: Competitive Rivalry — **HIGH**
The premium spirits industry is characterised by intense rivalry. Diageo competes directly with Pernod Ricard, Brown-Forman, Beam Suntory, and a proliferating number of craft distillers. The introduction of the Diageo Luxury Group in November 2024, targeting brands priced at $100 and above, signifies Diageo's premiumisation strategy and intent to differentiate from competitors. However, rivals are pursuing identical premiumisation strategies, intensifying competition at the high end.

### Force 2: Threat of New Entrants — **MODERATE**
While capital requirements and brand heritage create significant barriers, the craft spirits boom has lowered entry costs in niche categories. Mergers and acquisitions remain central to Diageo's growth strategy — recent deals include Don Papa Rum (~$280m) and Aviation Gin (~$610m); Diageo is actively pursuing tequila and non-alcoholic brands to match shifting consumer trends. This acquisition activity reflects both opportunity and the threat posed by agile new entrants.

### Force 3: Bargaining Power of Suppliers — **MODERATE**
Diageo sources agricultural products from a concentrated supplier base, creating dependency in categories such as agave (for tequila) and barley (for scotch). Climate change and crop volatility add further pressure to input costs.

### Force 4: Bargaining Power of Buyers — **HIGH**
Walmart, Costco, and Tesco control 59.2% of global alcohol retail distribution channels. These retailers negotiate prices aggressively, demanding volume discounts. Simultaneously, informed consumers are increasingly price-sensitive, especially in a cost-of-living squeeze.

### Force 5: Threat of Substitutes — **HIGH and Rising**
Social and economic trends markedly influence consumer behaviour regarding alcoholic beverages. The impact of health consciousness has led to an increase in demand for low-alcohol and non-alcoholic options. Cannabis beverages, functional wellness drinks, and premium RTD (ready-to-drink) products represent credible substitutes for traditional spirits occasions.

**Overall Industry Attractiveness:** Despite structural pressures, the long-term fundamentals remain positive. The key strategic imperative is to defend premium positioning while adapting to behavioural shifts.

## 5. Internal Analysis: SWOT

The SWOT framework (Andrews, 1971) synthesises the internal and external analyses.

### Strengths
- Diageo's competitive edge is built on a robust portfolio of 13 billion-dollar brands, an extensive global reach, and a well-established distribution network.
- Strategic priorities include maintaining leadership in whisk(e)y and tequila, expanding Guinness globally, and capitalising on the moderation trend with non-alcoholic offerings.
- In North America, Diageo outperformed the market with high quality share growth and positive organic net sales growth, driven by strong execution and momentum in Don Julio and Crown Royal. Guinness delivered double-digit growth for an eighth consecutive half, supported by brand building expertise, innovation and growing global momentum.

### Weaknesses
- Heavy geographic concentration risk, with North America accounting for ~40% of revenues and over half of group profits.
- Organic operating margin declined by 130bps in FY2024, primarily driven by the LAC region and an increase in investments in strategic initiatives.
- Slow pivot to the no/low alcohol category relative to the scale of the moderation trend.

### Opportunities
- Emerging markets: Diageo has the opportunity to tap into the growing middle class in emerging markets such as China, India, and Brazil. These markets offer significant growth potential for premium spirits and beverages, driven by increasing disposable incomes and changing consumer preferences.
- Diageo's non-alcoholic portfolio is up by about 56%, far outperforming other sectors of the business, with Ritual cited as a driving force.
- Non-alcoholic & RTD expansion: expanded Guinness 0.0 and Tanqueray 0.0 in 2025 after 15% year-on-year demand growth for premium alcohol-free alternatives.

### Threats
- An approximate US$150 million unmitigated annual impact from US tariffs on UK and European imports.
- Nearly half of Americans are trying to drink less alcohol in 2025, with interest in sober-curious lifestyles increasing 44% in just two years.
- Currency volatility and geopolitical instability across key emerging markets.

**Key Insight:** Diageo's core brands and distribution network constitute genuine, hard-to-replicate sources of sustained advantage. However, its no/low alcohol capability — a critical future battleground — is currently only at competitive parity. This is a strategic gap that requires urgent investment.

## 8. Business-Level Strategy: Porter's Generic Strategies

Diageo's overarching competitive strategy is **Differentiation** (Porter, 1985), pursued through premiumisation, brand heritage, and experiential marketing. The strategy of premiumisation, evident in offerings like Johnnie Walker Blue Label, aims to capture higher margins and enhance brand perception in the competitive spirits market. Diageo's broad portfolio, spanning various price points and categories, allows it to cater to a wide array of consumer needs and preferences.

However, there is a risk of **"stuck in the middle"** positioning as Diageo simultaneously:
1. Defends volume in mass-market categories (Smirnoff, Captain Morgan)
2. Chases ultra-premium growth (Diageo Luxury Group, $100+ brands)
3. Explores the entirely new no/low alcohol segment

The strategic divestment of lower-margin assets and a sharper focus on core premium brands highlight Diageo's intent to prioritise high-margin growth, even amid ongoing global uncertainty. This is the correct strategic direction, but execution must accelerate.

## 11. Strategic Recommendations & Action Plan

Drawing on the above analysis, the following five strategic recommendations are proposed, structured using the **SMART** objective framework and prioritised by urgency and impact.

---

### Recommendation 1: Accelerate the No/Low Alcohol Portfolio — *From Question Mark to Star*
**Rationale:** The VRIO analysis identified no/low alcohol as only at competitive parity. The moderation mega-trend is structural, not cyclical. Diageo's non-alcoholic portfolio is already up by about 56%, far outperforming other sectors of the business — this momentum must be scaled urgently.

**Action Plan:**
- Increase R&D and marketing investment in Guinness 0.0, Tanqueray 0.0, Seedlip, and Ritual by at least 30% over two years
- Create a dedicated "NoLo Innovation Lab" within Diageo to fast-track product development
- Target distribution parity with alcoholic equivalents in top-10 markets by 2027
- Acquire or partner with 1–2 functional wellness beverage brands (adaptogens, CBD) to future-proof the portfolio

**Strategic Logic:** Ansoff Product Development + BCG Question Mark to Star conversion

---

### Recommendation 2: Reduce US Tariff Exposure Through Supply Chain Localisation
**Rationale:** Diageo removed its medium-term guidance due to macroeconomic and geopolitical uncertainty, with US tariffs potentially resulting in a $200 million hit to second-half operating profit, primarily from disrupted tequila sales. This is an existential risk to the company's most profitable market.

**Action Plan:**
- Accelerate investment in US-based distilling and bottling capacity for key imported brands (particularly Scotch blends and gin)
- Expand the Don Julio agave sourcing and production footprint in Mexico to reduce cross-border exposure
- Lobby actively through industry bodies (DISCUS) for tariff exemptions on premium spirits
- Develop a dual-sourcing model for top-selling SKUs to enable rapid supply chain pivoting

**Strategic Logic:** Risk mitigation; Porter's Value Chain reconfiguration; operational resilience

---

### Recommendation 3: Rebalance Geographic Revenue Dependency — Prioritise Africa and India
**Rationale:** North America's ~40% revenue contribution creates dangerous concentration risk. Diageo's strategy in Africa is to grow beers fast and spirits faster, building resilience, agility and strength into its African businesses as they develop. Africa and India represent the most compelling long-term structural growth opportunities.

**Action Plan:**
- Increase capital allocation to Africa by 20% over three years, focusing on Nigeria (post-restructuring), Kenya, and South Africa
- Scale up manufacturing in India (Rajasthan) and launch India-tailored luxury brand variants to address rising middle-class disposable income
- Develop Africa-specific price-tier products to capture the mass-premium segment without cannibalising global brands
- Pursue joint ventures and local partnerships (as demonstrated by the Nigeria model) to navigate regulatory complexity

**Strategic Logic:** Ansoff Market Development; BCG portfolio rebalancing; geographic risk diversification

---

### Recommendation 4: Sharpen the Premium Core — Divest Non-Core Assets and Protect Margins
**Rationale:** Diageo's differentiation strategy risks dilution if the portfolio remains too broad. The strategic divestment of lower-margin assets and a sharper focus on core premium brands highlight Diageo's intent to prioritise high-margin growth.

**Action Plan:**
- Complete the divestment of non-core, sub-premium brands within 18 months, redeploying capital to premium and super-premium categories
- Invest behind the Diageo Luxury Group (brands priced $100+) with bespoke retail experiences, collector editions, and NFT-backed provenance authentication
- Increase premium-plus brands' contribution to total net sales from 63% to 68% for the fiscal year — maintain this as a KPI tracked quarterly
- Protect Johnnie Walker's brand equity through tighter supply management and experiential marketing (e.g., Johnnie Walker Houses)

**Strategic Logic:** Porter's Differentiation strategy; VRIO sustained competitive advantage; BCG Cash Cow optimisation

---

### Recommendation 5: Build Digital and Data Capabilities as a Core Competency
**Rationale:** The VRIO analysis showed digital/data analytics as only at competitive parity. In an era of direct-to-consumer commerce and personalised marketing, this is insufficient.

**Action Plan:**
- Invest in a proprietary first-party consumer data platform, integrating e-commerce, on-trade, and loyalty programme data
- Deploy AI-driven demand forecasting to prevent inventory crises like the LAC situation (which cost Diageo $310 million in FY2024)
- Accelerate digital-first campaign experiences, leveraging data analytics to drive luxury positioning and market share growth
- Partner with major platforms (Amazon, Alibaba) to build D2C channels in key markets, reducing dependency on the retail oligopoly

**Strategic Logic:** Resource-Based View (Barney, 1991); VRIO capability building; competitive parity to advantage

## 13. Conclusion

Diageo occupies an enviable strategic position — 13 billion-dollar brands, 180-country distribution, and category leadership in the world's fastest-growing spirits segments. Yet the convergence of US tariffs, the moderation mega-trend, geographic revenue concentration, and digital disruption represents a genuine multi-front strategic challenge that demands urgent, coordinated action.

Organic net sales growth of 1.7%, driven by organic volume growth of 0.9% and positive price/mix of 0.8%, with Don Julio, Guinness and Crown Royal as standout performers, shows that whilst there is clearly more to do, Diageo is showing organic growth ahead of competition. This is a platform to build from, not rest on.

The frameworks applied — PESTEL, Porter's Five Forces, SWOT, VRIO, Ansoff, BCG, and Porter's Generic Strategies — collectively point to the same strategic imperative: **double down on the premium core, aggressively build no/low alcohol capability, reduce geographic and tariff concentration risk, and invest in digital as a competitive weapon.** Executed with discipline, these recommendations position Diageo not merely to survive the current headwinds, but to emerge structurally stronger — a company that leads the next era of the global drinks industry, whether consumers choose to drink alcohol or not.
