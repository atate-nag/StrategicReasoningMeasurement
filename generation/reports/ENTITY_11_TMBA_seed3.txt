# 📋 STRATEGIC MANAGEMENT CASE STUDY
## *"Diamonds Under Pressure: De Beers and the Crisis of Identity in the Modern Diamond Industry"*
### An Anglo American Subsidiary in Strategic Transition

> **Frameworks Applied:** PESTLE · Porter's Five Forces · SWOT · Value Chain · BCG Matrix · Ansoff Matrix · SAFe Criteria · Cultural Web

---

## EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

De Beers Group, the world's most iconic diamond company and an 85%-owned subsidiary of Anglo American plc, finds itself at a profound strategic crossroads. Once commanding over 80% of the global rough diamond supply, De Beers has seen its market share erode to approximately 29% by 2023. Revenue declined 23% year-over-year to $3.29 billion in 2024, with rough diamond sales falling 25% to $2.7 billion. Anglo American has written down the value of De Beers for the second year in a row, taking a $2.9 billion impairment charge in 2024. In May 2024, Anglo American announced its intention to spin off or sell De Beers as part of a "radical" restructuring. The twin forces of lab-grown diamond (LGD) disruption and shifting generational consumer values have exposed deep structural vulnerabilities in De Beers' century-old business model.

---

## SECTION 1: COMPANY BACKGROUND AND CONTEXT

### 1.1 Anglo American and De Beers

Anglo American plc, headquartered in London, is one of the world's largest diversified mining companies. The company is currently owned 85% by Anglo American and 15% by the Government of Botswana. In May 2024, Anglo American announced its intention to spin off or sell De Beers.

Through Debswana, the 50-50 joint venture between the Botswana government and De Beers, the country has maintained significant operational involvement in diamond mining activities. De Beers also operates the Forevermark and De Beers Jewellers retail brands, and until 2025, operated Lightbox — its lab-grown diamond brand launched in 2018.

### 1.2 The Strategic Situation: A Confluence of Crises

Between 2022 and 2025, De Beers experienced a perfect storm of structural, competitive, and macroeconomic challenges:

- Revenue declined 23% year-over-year to $3.29 billion in 2024, with rough diamond sales falling 25% to $2.7 billion.
- Rough diamond production was reduced by 22% to 24.7 million carats (2023: 31.9 million carats), reflecting a proactive production response to a prolonged period of lower demand and higher than normal levels of inventory in the midstream.
- Underlying EBITDA swung from $72 million positive in 2023 to negative $25 million in 2024 — a dramatic reversal.
- Seven years after its introduction sent shock waves through the industry, De Beers closed the Lightbox lab-grown diamond business in May 2025.
- Anglo's decision to exit diamonds came after successfully defending against BHP Group's £39 billion hostile takeover bid in 2024, prompting a radical restructuring focused on future-facing metals.

## SECTION 2: EXTERNAL ENVIRONMENT ANALYSIS

### 2.1 PESTLE Analysis

#### 🔵 Political
The ownership structure of De Beers places Anglo American in the dominant position with an 85% stake, while Botswana holds a 15% minority position. Botswana has increasingly positioned itself to gain majority control of De Beers. New G7 diamond import rules now require provenance documentation, creating both a compliance burden and a strategic opportunity for De Beers' Tracr blockchain platform.

#### 🔴 Economic
A key driver of growth concern was not demand but a strategic decision to break with De Beers' usual policy of withholding supply during downturns and instead sell less desirable rough in special large-volume transactions to sightholders. De Beers' rough price index dropped 12% excluding these "stock rebalancing initiatives."

#### 🟡 Social
Millennials and Gen Z now drive two-thirds of diamond demand. Gen Z consumers, who prioritize authenticity, sustainability, and social impact, demand more than just a product; they seek a brand that reflects their own values. The majority (60%) of U.S. Millennial and Gen-Z women search the Internet prior to purchasing a diamond.

#### 🟢 Technological
The Tracr platform utilises blockchain, artificial intelligence, the internet of things and advanced security and privacy technologies in order to track diamonds from source to store. The platform now has over three million diamonds registered at source.

Lab-grown diamond jewellery sales neared $9 billion in 2024. The US is by far the largest market for lab-grown diamonds, accounting for 70% of global sales of lab-grown diamond jewellery in 2024.

#### ⚫ Environmental
An impairment of $2.3 billion (before tax and non-controlling interests) to Anglo American's carrying value of De Beers has been recognised, driven by lower forecasted prices than previously, due to greater shifting of customer preference between natural diamonds and laboratory-grown diamonds, and surplus of available rough diamonds relative to prevailing demand.

### 2.2 Porter's Five Forces Analysis

| Force | Rating | Key Driver |
|---|---|---|
| **Threat of New Entrants** | LOW–MODERATE | High mining capex; but LGD entry barriers are low |
| **Supplier Power** | LOW | De Beers is vertically integrated |
| **Buyer Power** | **HIGH** | Multiple diamond sources; informed consumers; LGD price comparisons |
| **Threat of Substitutes** | **VERY HIGH** | LGDs are chemically identical at a fraction of the price |
| **Competitive Rivalry** | **HIGH** | ALROSA (31% share) vs De Beers (29%); declining market |

**Threat of Substitutes** is the defining force. Premium lab-grown diamond stones have demonstrated price resilience, declining only 10–15% in 2024 compared to 40–50% for commercial goods — meaning even the "premium" LGD segment is commoditising rapidly. The availability of substitute products or services makes the competitive environment challenging for De Beers. High substitute threat shows that customers can use alternative products.

**Buyer Power** is amplified by digital transparency. Customers could compare carat prices across lab diamonds with ease. They could understand cost vs markup in a way that's rarely possible with mined stones.

## SECTION 6: STRATEGIC RECOMMENDATIONS & ACTION PLAN

### HORIZON 1: STABILISE (0–18 Months) — Stop the Bleeding

#### ▶ Recommendation 1.1: Conclude the Ownership Transition
**Action:** Anglo American must conclude the De Beers divestment without further delay. The preferred outcome is a structured sale to a consortium anchored by the Botswana government (increasing its stake meaningfully), sovereign wealth funds, and luxury goods investors.

**Rationale:** Plans to sell or demerge De Beers, the loss-making diamond company, were first announced in May 2024 as part of a "radical" restructuring at Anglo. Strategic paralysis under ownership uncertainty is preventing the long-term investment commitments required for renewal.

**KPI:** Divestment/demerger completed by end of 2025.

#### ▶ Recommendation 1.2: Right-Size Production for Margin, Not Volume
**Action:** Maintain production discipline at approximately 24.7 million carats, prioritising high-value, gem-quality stones from Jwaneng — the world's richest diamond mine by value — over volume.

**Rationale:** Flooding the market with rough diamonds has proven counterproductive. The BCG Cash Cow must be managed for margin.

**KPI:** Rough price index stabilisation; EBITDA return to positive within 18 months.

#### ▶ Recommendation 1.3: Clear the Midstream Inventory Overhang
**Action:** Work collaboratively with sightholders to develop structured inventory reduction plans, including flexible sight terms and targeted promotional support.

**KPI:** Midstream inventory normalised within 12 months.

---

### HORIZON 2: DIFFERENTIATE (18 Months – 3 Years) — Build the Moat

#### ▶ Recommendation 2.1: Relaunch Category Marketing — "Born of the Earth, Irreplaceable"
**Action:** Reinvest significantly in category-level marketing ($200M+ annually) with a campaign architecture that:
- Emphasises the **4-billion-year geological journey** of natural diamonds — a narrative no lab can replicate
- Targets **Millennials and Gen Z** through digital-first, social-media-led campaigns aligned with their values of authenticity and self-expression
- Repositions diamonds **beyond the engagement ring** — celebrating familial love, self-purchase, and life milestones
- Partners with luxury fashion houses and digital creators to embed natural diamonds in aspirational lifestyle narratives

**Rationale:** De Beers has a track record for driving growth in demand for natural diamonds through category marketing. De Beers, the company that essentially created the modern diamond engagement ring tradition, launched the "Real is Rare" campaign specifically to counter the growing popularity of lab-grown diamonds. A bolder, better-funded version of this approach is now required.

**KPI:** Consumer perception scores for natural diamond uniqueness; year-on-year increase in natural diamond retail sales among target demographics.

#### ▶ Recommendation 2.2: Scale Tracr as the Global Diamond Provenance Standard
**Action:**
- Mandate Tracr registration for **all** De Beers-produced diamonds
- Develop a **consumer-facing Tracr certificate** — a QR-code-enabled digital passport accessible at point of sale
- **License Tracr** to other natural diamond producers as a B2B SaaS platform, establishing it as the industry standard
- Leverage Tracr for G7 compliance as a competitive advantage in Western markets

**Rationale:** Tracr's technology is not only valuable for registering the information required under new diamond import rules within G7 countries, but also offers an enhanced consumer proposition through connecting diamonds to their source. Each diamond registered on the platform is assigned a unique digital identity, and every transaction or process it undergoes is recorded on the blockchain, creating a secure and auditable trail.

**KPI:** 10M+ diamonds registered on Tracr within 3 years; Tracr licensed to 5+ external producers.

#### ▶ Recommendation 2.3: Premiumise the Forevermark Brand
**Action:** Reposition Forevermark as the De Beers consumer-facing premium brand with:
- Every stone Tracr-certified and Forevermark-inscribed
- A redesigned **phygital retail experience** (physical + digital) immersing consumers in the provenance narrative
- Partnerships with global jewellery retailers (Signet, Chow Tai Fook, Tanishq) for dedicated shop-in-shop experiences
- A direct-to-consumer e-commerce platform with personalised diamond discovery tools

**KPI:** Forevermark retail sales growth of 15%+ per year; NPS improvement.

---

### HORIZON 3: GROW (3–5 Years) — Capture New Markets

#### ▶ Recommendation 3.1: Accelerate India and Southeast Asia Market Development
**Action:** Establish dedicated marketing and trade development teams in India, Vietnam, Thailand, and Indonesia, partnering with local jewellery brands and leveraging cultural gifting occasions (Diwali, weddings, coming-of-age ceremonies).

**Rationale:** This demographic is highly digitally fluent, and their purchasing decisions are heavily influenced by social media, online reviews, and peer recommendations. India's growing middle class and deep cultural affinity for diamond jewellery represent a structural growth opportunity.

**KPI:** 20%+ revenue growth from India and Southeast Asia within 5 years.

#### ▶ Recommendation 3.2: Commercialise Tracr Beyond Diamonds
**Action:** License Tracr's blockchain provenance technology to adjacent luxury goods sectors — coloured gemstones, gold, platinum, and luxury fashion — creating a diversified technology revenue stream.

**KPI:** Tracr technology revenue of $50M+ per year within 5 years.

#### ▶ Recommendation 3.3: Embed ESG as a Competitive Advantage
**Action:**
- Accelerate transition to renewable energy across all mining operations, targeting net-zero Scope 1 and 2 emissions by 2030
- Publish a detailed, third-party-verified "Natural Diamond Sustainability Report" annually, benchmarking De Beers' environmental performance against LGD producers
- Make community development programmes in Botswana, South Africa, and Namibia a prominent part of the brand narrative

**Rationale:** Lab-grown diamonds have seen a surge in popularity due to their ethical appeal and cost-effectiveness. As consumers become more conscious of environmental and ethical issues, the demand for sustainable alternatives has increased. De Beers must challenge the LGD "sustainable" narrative with verifiable evidence of responsible mining's community impact.

**KPI:** Renewable energy share; third-party ESG ratings; consumer awareness of De Beers' sustainability commitments.

## SECTION 9: CONCLUSION

De Beers stands at a historic inflection point. The forces arrayed against it — lab-grown diamond disruption, generational value shifts, macroeconomic headwinds, and ownership uncertainty — are real and structural. However, De Beers possesses assets that no competitor can replicate: a 135-year brand legacy, the world's richest diamond mines, a first-mover blockchain provenance platform, and a proven ability to shape consumer desire at a global scale.

The strategic path forward is **not** to compete with lab-grown diamonds on price — that battle is lost. Nor is it to retreat into operational conservatism. The path forward is **bold differentiation**: to make natural diamonds so compelling, so traceable, so emotionally resonant, and so demonstrably unique that no lab-grown alternative can substitute for them in the minds of the world's most discerning consumers.

The frameworks applied in this analysis — PESTLE, Porter's Five Forces, SWOT, Value Chain, BCG Matrix, Ansoff Matrix, SAFe Criteria, and the Cultural Web — converge on a consistent diagnosis: De Beers' crisis is fundamentally a **strategic identity crisis**. The company built its empire on scarcity and supply control. In the twenty-first century, it must rebuild it on **provenance, purpose, and desire**.

The diamond, as De Beers itself once proclaimed, is forever. The company's future depends on making consumers believe it again.
