# STRATEGIC CASE STUDY: Kumba Iron Ore Limited (Anglo American Subsidiary)
## *The Logistics Trap — Navigating Infrastructure Dependency, Value Erosion, and Strategic Renewal*

---

## THE STRATEGIC SITUATION

Following a strategic review in 2023, Kumba reconfigured its business to an overall lower production profile of 35–37 Mt for the period 2024–2026, in line with prevailing logistics capacity. This reconfiguration was not triggered by mine-level failure. Kumba's total production decreased by 2% to 18.5Mt in H1 2024, in line with Transnet's rail performance, reflecting a 12% decrease in Kolomela's production and a 3% increase at Sishen. The constraint lay entirely downstream of the mine gate.

South Africa's freight rail network reached peak demand of 226.3 million tonnes in 2017, but by 2022, volumes had fallen by 34% to a historic low of 149.5 million tonnes, primarily due to deteriorating network performance characterised by delays, poor maintenance, equipment shortages, cable theft, and vandalism. For Kumba, this had direct financial consequences: the business reconfiguration and proactive cost optimisation realised savings of R4.4 billion, contributing positively to an adjusted EBITDA of R28.1 billion, at an adjusted EBITDA margin of 41% — down sharply from R45.7 billion EBITDA and an EBITDA margin of 53% in 2023.

---

## FRAMEWORK 1: PORTER'S FIVE FORCES

The critical force is **supplier power**. Transnet operated at around 80% of its contractual volumes for Kumba in 2024 — its worst level yet, which had a significant knock-on effect on Kumba's production and sales volumes. There is no alternative rail route and no alternative export port — a structural monopoly that converts Transnet's institutional failures directly into Kumba's financial losses.

On **buyer power**, Kumba's relatively high iron content of about 64% is nicely ahead of competitors, earning the full Fe premium on top of that. This quality premium is the primary lever against buyer power, but the sharp decrease in iron ore prices — which were at US$100/tonne by the end of December 2024, down from a high of US$143/tonne in January 2024 — caused Kumba's average realised price to decrease by 21% to US$92/wmt.

On **substitutes**, Kumba's high-grade ore is well-positioned: around 97% of Kumba's total emissions are Scope 3 emissions, with most relating to the processing of sold products (iron and steelmaking), and the company is working with Anglo American to drive a decarbonised "green steel" value chain. Higher Fe content means less energy and fewer emissions per tonne of steel — making Kumba a preferred supplier in a carbon-constrained world.

---

## FRAMEWORK 2: RESOURCE-BASED VIEW (VRIN)

Kumba's ore body is **Valuable, Rare, Inimitable, and Non-substitutable**. Kumba's reserves support a life-of-mine extending beyond 20 years at current production rates, while prioritising high-grade lump and premium products.

The UHDMS investment directly deepens this VRIN advantage: UHDMS technology is expected to increase the volume of premium iron ore to approximately 55% of Sishen's production, up from the current approximately 18%, expected to increase the product premium by US$2–3/t on average above lump and Fe premiums. The R11.2 billion total investment in the UHDMS technology is expected to result in an EBITDA margin of more than 50% and an IRR of over 30%.

Kumba's **social licence** is also a VRIN resource in the South African context. Continued transformation and investing in skills and supplier development, as well as local procurement spend, contributed to Kumba's B-BBEE scorecard advancing to Level 5 in 2024, a progressive improvement from Level 6 in 2023 and Level 7 in 2022.

---

## FRAMEWORK 3: SWOT ANALYSIS

**Key Strength**: Kumba is a renowned lump iron-ore producer, and major iron-ore consumer China's steel mills are beginning to use more lump, which strengthens Kumba's position.

**Key Weakness**: Rail performance was impacted by several derailments and locomotive breakdowns, resulting in ore railed to port decreasing by 2% to 18.1Mt in H1 2024, with total finished stock remaining high at 8.2Mt.

**Key Opportunity**: Transnet's Network Statement, issued in December 2024, represents an important step forward by facilitating third-party access, and Kumba is continuing to engage actively with Transnet and government to explore opportunities for a private sector participation concessionaire model.

**Key Threat**: Despite having "a superb ore-body and mining as well as processing infrastructure, Kumba has been badly let down" by Transnet, with pressure on the Chinese steel market, Chinese dumping, tariffs on Chinese goods, and no near-term Transnet improvement suggesting continued pressure.

---

## FRAMEWORK 4: STAKEHOLDER MAPPING (Mendelow)

The most critical stakeholder relationships converge on the logistics problem. Among regulatory progress made in 2024 to restore Transnet's viability, was work to develop a concessionaire model, under which private sector parties would have operational and maintenance responsibilities for the OEC (ore export channel) from the Northern Cape to Saldanha Bay, for a defined period, once approved by government.

On workforce: the Social, Ethics and Transformation Committee was, through 2024, particularly engaged in monitoring the group reconfiguration plan, reviewing the measures taken to support the 688 permanent employees directly affected by the reconfiguration. Kumba Iron Ore issued retrenchment notices that could affect 1,600 employees, in a process that trade union Solidarity said could result in 653 job losses. These retrenchments risk destabilising the government relationship at precisely the moment when government support for logistics reform is most needed.

---

## FRAMEWORK 5: ANSOFF MATRIX

**Option 1 — Market Penetration (Logistics Restoration)**: In Q4 2024, the rail performance tempo improved due to speed restrictions being lifted on 50km of the OEC following an upgrade on 90km of rail infrastructure — demonstrating that incremental improvement is achievable, and that full market penetration can be restored through structural reform.

**Option 2 — Product Development (UHDMS)**: The first UHDMS module is targeted for production in 2026, with the main plant tie-in scheduled for that year as well. Full conversion and ramp-up to steady-state operation are expected by the end of 2028. This is the most strategically robust option because it is largely within management's control.

**Option 3 — Market Development**: Strong steel margins underpinned Chinese iron ore demand in the third quarter of 2025, keeping prices elevated despite ample supply of steel — but India's projected long-term steel demand growth makes market development a sensible supplementary risk-mitigation measure.
