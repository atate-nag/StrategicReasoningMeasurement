## 🏢 THE STRATEGIC SITUATION

The case centres on Diageo's convergence of **cyclical and structural crises**:
- A **23% revenue collapse in Latin America & Caribbean (LAC)** driven by a post-pandemic inventory glut
- **Declining youth alcohol consumption** — the "sober-curious" Gen Z movement
- **Softening North American volumes** due to weak consumer confidence
- **High leverage (3.4× net debt/EBITDA)** limiting strategic flexibility
- **Withdrawal of medium-term guidance** in FY2025, signalling the old strategy is failing

## 🎯 THE RECOMMENDED STRATEGY: THE DUAL-ENGINE MODEL

### Pillar 1 — Fix the Foundation (0–18 months)
- Deploy AI-driven demand-sensing across all distributor channels
- Reduce leverage below 3.0× through the Accelerate savings programme and selective brand divestitures
- Restructure the LAC go-to-market with tighter inventory controls

### Pillar 2 — Defend the Premium Core (0–36 months)
- Disproportionate investment behind **Don Julio and Casamigos** (tequila's structural tailwinds)
- Revitalise **Johnnie Walker** through experiential marketing and flavour innovation
- Accelerate **Guinness** as a global brand (not just a regional heritage asset)

### Pillar 3 — Build the Second Engine: NoLo & Digital (12–48 months)
- Create a **dedicated NoLo business unit** with its own P&L and innovation budget
- Pursue **targeted NoLo acquisitions** in premium adult soft drinks and functional beverages
- Build **DTC and social commerce** capabilities targeting Gen Z and Millennials
- Scale **GenAI marketing tools** to reduce content costs and personalise at scale

### Pillar 4 — ESG as Competitive Advantage (Ongoing)
- Accelerate "Spirit of Progress" — 50% Scope 1&2 emissions reduction by 2030
- Embed sustainability into brand storytelling to resonate with values-driven Gen Z consumers
