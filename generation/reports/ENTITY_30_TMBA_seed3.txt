# POLESTAR: NAVIGATING THE CROSSROADS
## A Strategic Case Study

---

## EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

Polestar Automotive Holding UK PLC (Nasdaq: PSNY) is a Swedish premium electric vehicle manufacturer caught at a strategic crossroads. Polestar, originally a racing car manufacturer and tuning subsidiary of Volvo, has evolved into a standalone electric performance brand. Principally owned by Li Shufu's PSD Investment, Geely Holding and Volvo Cars, the company is headquartered in Torslanda, outside Gothenburg, Sweden. Its central strategic tension — Swedish brand identity, Chinese ownership, global premium ambition — defines both its greatest asset and its most acute vulnerability.

---

## PART 1: COMPANY BACKGROUND

### 1.1 Corporate Structure

Polestar is jointly owned by Volvo Cars and Geely, a large automotive group based in China. Volvo itself is also owned by Geely, which helps explain why the connection between the brands is so strong. This arrangement allows Polestar to benefit from Volvo's established manufacturing infrastructure.

### 1.2 Product Portfolio

Polestar plans to have a line-up of five performance EVs by 2026: the Polestar 2 (electric performance fastback, launched in 2019), Polestar 3 (the SUV for the electric age, launched in late 2022), Polestar 4 (the SUV coupé, launching through 2023 and into 2024), and Polestar 5 (an electric four-door Grand Tourer). By 2028, Polestar also plans to bring the Polestar 7 — a premium compact SUV — to market.

### 1.3 The Strategic Situation

Polestar reported a gross loss of $414.7 million for FY2023, representing a significant shift from a profit of $98.4 million in 2022, with a gross margin of negative 17.4% compared with a positive 4% in 2022. Swedish EV maker Polestar reported a decrease in 2023 revenue and widened losses due to slowing demand for its higher-priced models. Its U.S.-listed shares dropped, contributing to a year-to-date slump of over 63%.

Yet simultaneously, Polestar closed 2025 with its strongest retail performance to date, delivering 60,119 vehicles worldwide — a 34% year-over-year increase — despite pricing pressure, slower EV adoption in some regions, and tighter capital markets.

The central strategic question is: **Can Polestar translate its brand identity and product vision into a viable, profitable, and globally competitive business — and if so, how?**

## PART 2: EXTERNAL ENVIRONMENT — PESTEL ANALYSIS

### Political
Polestar warned that proposed U.S. regulations on Chinese software in cars could put it out of business, as a proposed set of rules meant to protect national security "would effectively" prohibit its U.S. sales. The Polestar 4 faces ill feelings toward China post-COVID, the stagnation of the EV market, and 25% import tariffs at a time when Canadian and U.S. EV government subsidies are going away.

### Economic
High interest rates have been pushing consumers towards more affordable hybrid options. Challenges in the domestic Chinese market include overcapacity and severe price wars that have eroded profitability, with the median net profit margin for listed Chinese automakers falling to 0.83% in 2024.

### Social
The Polestar 3, positioned as a luxury electric SUV with sports car performance and a starting price of $73,400, exemplifies the company's strategy to compete in the high-end segment. Consumer environmental consciousness is driving market growth, with 73% of global consumers willing to pay a premium for sustainable products.

### Technological
Powertrain upgrades for the 2024 model year increased the Polestar 2's range by 20%, cut its charging time by nearly 35%, and reduced its overall carbon footprint. Polestar's reliance on a restricted pool of specialized EV battery suppliers elevates supplier power — a crucial dependency given the complex technology in their EVs.

### Environmental
The Polestar 0 Project aims to eliminate all greenhouse gas emissions from every aspect of the supply chain and production — from conception all the way to customer delivery — without resorting to offsetting by tree planting or other carbon credit schemes. Polestar's 2024 Sustainability Report highlighted a 24.7% reduction in carbon emissions per vehicle and noted that the company has shaved approximately 3 tonnes of CO₂ from the Polestar 2's lifecycle footprint since 2021.

### Legal
The company first encountered a Nasdaq minimum bid price issue on July 5, 2024, when its American Depositary Shares traded below the $1.00 threshold for 30 consecutive trading days. The cycle of non-compliance reflects both financial fragility and the legal complexity of its cross-border listing structure.

Polestar's primary direct competitor is Tesla, particularly with models like the Model 3 and Model Y. Tesla's strong brand presence and extensive Supercharger network provide a significant advantage. Polestar competes differently — targeting buyers who want premium EV design, restrained styling, and traditional build quality. While Tesla focuses on scale efficiency, Polestar prioritises material quality, ride tuning, and brand clarity.

## PART 4: INTERNAL ANALYSIS — RBV & VRIO

The Resource-Based View (Barney, 1991) holds that sustainable competitive advantage derives from resources that are **Valuable, Rare, Inimitable, and Organised** (VRIO). Polestar's key resources assessed:

| Resource | V | R | I | O | Implication |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| Scandinavian design heritage | ✅ | ✅ | ✅ | ✅ | **Sustained Advantage** |
| Polestar 0 / Sustainability mission | ✅ | ✅ | Partial | ✅ | **Temporary Advantage** |
| Digital-first retail model | ✅ | Partial | ❌ | ✅ | **Competitive Parity** |
| Geely/Volvo platform access | ✅ | ❌ | ❌ | ✅ | **Competitive Parity** |
| Brand awareness (global) | Partial | ❌ | N/A | ❌ | **Disadvantage** |
| Financial resources | ❌ | ❌ | N/A | ❌ | **Disadvantage** |

Polestar's brand strategy focuses on the core tenets of design, sustainability, and innovative technology — a foundation for its brand image that raises awareness among new audiences. Polestar Spaces emphasise minimalist architecture and product education, reinforcing the brand's quiet-luxury positioning.

**Key Insight**: Polestar's only *sustained* competitive advantage is its **design identity and sustainability ethos** — rare, inimitable, and genuinely differentiated. This must be the strategic anchor for all future decisions.

## PART 5: SWOT ANALYSIS

### Strengths
- Distinctive Scandinavian design language and "quiet luxury" brand identity
- Polestar has emerged as a trailblazer in the automotive industry, pushing the boundaries of what's possible in sustainable mobility through the Polestar 0 project.
- Access to Geely/Volvo manufacturing ecosystem and shared platforms
- Polestar created an entirely new buying process — a digital-first strategy that allows customers to learn about, purchase, and receive their car without ever stepping foot into a dealership.

### Weaknesses
- Chronic financial losses and negative gross margins
- Polestar has significant challenges generating brand awareness and consideration in the United States.
- Manufacturing concentration in China creating tariff and geopolitical exposure
- Small production scale limits economies of scale and supplier negotiating power

### Opportunities
- The global EV market is projected to reach $957.4 billion by 2028, with a CAGR of 18.2%.
- SUVs now account for 56% of Polestar's orders, aligning with the new Polestar 3, 4, and 7 product roadmap
- Polestar is implementing a 75% expansion of its global retail presence by 2026 and the launch of sales operations in France.

### Threats
- EU and U.S. import tariffs on Polestar's Chinese-made cars are becoming a significant issue, requiring "mitigating measures" to achieve positive cash flow.
- Intensifying competition from legacy OEMs and Chinese EV brands
- Consumer shift towards hybrids in high-interest-rate environment

## PART 6: STRATEGIC RECOMMENDATIONS

### Recommendation 1: Ruthlessly Prioritise Design and Sustainability as the Core Differentiator
*(Timeframe: Immediate | Framework: VRIO / RBV)*

Polestar's only sustained competitive advantage is its design heritage and sustainability mission. It must deepen and monetise this — not compete on Tesla's technology-first terms.

**Actions:**
- Develop a **"Polestar Provenance" digital passport** for every vehicle, tracing the carbon footprint of every component from raw material to delivery — turning sustainability transparency into a premium product feature
- Commission independent third-party verification of lifecycle carbon data, published prominently on every vehicle
- Elevate the design leadership team to public-facing prominence, making Polestar's design philosophy as recognisable as its cars

### Recommendation 2: Achieve Financial Stability Through Disciplined Volume Growth
*(Timeframe: 0–12 months | Framework: Porter's Generic Strategies)*

CEO Michael Lohscheller has established aggressive growth targets including a 30–35% compound annual increase in retail sales volume over the next three years, building on the success of SUV models.

**Actions:**
- Focus volume growth on highest-margin models (Polestar 3 and 4 SUVs)
- Leverage Geely group purchasing power to renegotiate battery supply agreements
- Set a non-negotiable gross margin positivity target and communicate it transparently to capital markets

### Recommendation 3: Resolve the Identity Crisis — Become Unambiguously "Swedish"
*(Timeframe: Immediate–Medium | Framework: PESTEL — Political/Social)*

Volvo and Polestar are brands majority-owned and controlled by Chinese automaker Geely, putting the Swedish marques in a vortex of difficulty in the U.S. The brand must proactively manage this narrative.

**Actions:**
- Invest in a "Made in Sweden" narrative campaign emphasising the Gothenburg headquarters, Swedish design team, and Scandinavian values
- Leverage the new 80,000 m² Gothenburg headquarters as a flagship brand experience centre
- Publicly communicate the independence of Polestar's design and engineering decisions from Geely

### Recommendation 4: Diversify Manufacturing Away from China
*(Timeframe: 6–24 months | Framework: PESTEL — Political / Porter's Five Forces)*

Polestar's CEO has stated that manufacturing globalisation is driven not just by protectionism but by "logistic reasons, environmental reasons — you want to be as close as possible to the end market."

**Actions:**
- Accelerate production of Western-market vehicles at the South Carolina facility (Polestar 3)
- Explore European manufacturing partnerships for Polestar 5 and next-generation Polestar 2
- Use non-Chinese manufacturing as a commercial and political differentiator in tariff negotiations

### Recommendation 5: Focus Geographic Expansion on High-EV-Adoption Markets
*(Timeframe: 12–36 months | Framework: Ansoff's Market Development)*

Polestar expanded from 140 to 210 sales points during 2025 and is targeting 30% growth in sales points in 2026. But this expansion must be sequenced intelligently.

**Actions:**
- Prioritise Norway, Sweden, Netherlands, Germany, UK, and South Korea — markets with high EV adoption, supportive regulation, and minimal tariff barriers
- In the U.S., focus on high-density metropolitan markets (California, New York) rather than national coverage
- Defer expansion into markets requiring disproportionate investment until financial stability is achieved

### Recommendation 6: Transform Polestar 0 into a Revenue-Generating Platform
*(Timeframe: 24–60 months | Framework: Ansoff's Diversification / RBV)*

**Actions:**
- Develop a **"Polestar Sustainability Consulting"** capability, licensing the expertise developed through Polestar 0 to other industries facing CSRD reporting obligations
- Partner with premium non-automotive brands (luxury fashion, architecture) to co-create sustainability-focused products, extending the brand into lifestyle territory
- License the Polestar Provenance digital passport technology as a B2B SaaS product

### Recommendation 7: Build a Proprietary, Non-Chinese Software Strategy
*(Timeframe: 24–60 months | Framework: PESTEL — Technological / Competitive Rivalry)*

**Actions:**
- Deepen the existing Google Android Automotive OS partnership to develop a Polestar-specific software layer that is unambiguously non-Chinese in origin
- Develop OTA update capabilities that create an ongoing customer relationship and recurring revenue
- Build a connected vehicle data platform that upholds data transparency — a natural extension of the brand's sustainability ethos

## CONCLUSION

Polestar is a company of genuine strategic promise trapped in a cycle of financial fragility and identity ambiguity. Its sustained competitive advantage — a truly distinctive design identity and a credible, transparent sustainability mission — is rare and inimitable. Polestar stands at a pivotal crossroads, armed with a strong brand identity and an unwavering commitment to sustainability, yet challenged by production limitations and external market pressures.

The strategic recommendations presented here are not a radical reinvention — they are a disciplined amplification of what Polestar already does best, combined with the operational and financial rigour required to survive long enough to win. With 2025 expected to be "the strongest year in Polestar's history," the company's evolution from a niche premium EV maker to a sustainable, profitable global automotive brand hangs in the balance. The electric vehicle revolution is not a sprint; it is a marathon. Polestar has the brand DNA to run it — the question is whether it can find the financial oxygen to keep going.
