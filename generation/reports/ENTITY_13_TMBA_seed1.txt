# DIAGEO PLC: NAVIGATING THE PERFECT STORM
## A Strategic Case Study

---

## 1. EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

Diageo plc, the world's largest premium spirits company, faces a convergence of strategic challenges that threaten to erode its dominant market position. Fiscal 2024 was a year of challenge and change for Diageo as it navigated global economic and sectoral volatility, set new priorities, and appointed Directors to its Board. Between FY2023 and FY2025, the company experienced declining organic net sales in its most profitable market (North America), a catastrophic collapse in Latin America and the Caribbean (LAC), intensifying competition, and structural demand disruption from the rise of no/low-alcohol beverages and GLP-1 weight-loss drugs.

This case study applies PESTEL, Porter's Five Forces, SWOT, VRIO, the SAF Framework, Ansoff Matrix, and BCG Portfolio Analysis to diagnose the root causes of Diageo's underperformance and to develop a coherent, prioritised action plan.

## 2. COMPANY BACKGROUND

Diageo plc was formed in 1997 through the merger of Guinness plc and Grand Metropolitan. Headquartered in London and listed on both the London Stock Exchange and New York Stock Exchange, Diageo is a global leader in Total Beverage Alcohol (TBA) and one of the world's most successful brand builders, with an ambition to create one of the best performing, most trusted and respected consumer products companies in the world.

Its brands include Johnnie Walker, Crown Royal, J&B, Buchanan's whiskies, Smirnoff, Cîroc and Ketel One vodkas, Captain Morgan, Baileys, Don Julio, Tanqueray and Guinness, and its products are sold in nearly 180 countries.

**Financial Snapshot (FY2024, year ended 30 June 2024):**
- Net Sales: ~$20 billion (down 1.4% on FY2023)
- Market Capitalisation: ~$45 billion
- Employees: ~28,000

North America accounts for ~40% of revenues and more than half of group profits, making it Diageo's most strategically critical geography.

## 3. THE STRATEGIC SITUATION

### 3.1 The Triggering Events

**Crisis 1 – The North American Inventory Hangover (FY2023–FY2024)**

Price/mix grew 2pps but was more than offset by a 4% decline in volume. US Spirits net sales declined 3%, driven by a volume decline of 5%, with depletion growth approximately one percentage point ahead of shipment growth. This "inventory correction" — where distributors ran down pandemic-era over-stocking rather than placing new orders — caused widespread earnings pressure.

**Crisis 2 – The LAC Collapse (FY2024)**

Diageo pointed towards inventory replenishment in North America, the "volatile" market in the Latin America and Caribbean (LAC) region, and an underestimation of the current consumer environment as the key factors behind its sales drop. The LAC region experienced a significant downturn, with organic net sales falling by 21.1% in FY24, heavily impacting overall company performance.

**Crisis 3 – Structural Demand Disruption**

GLP-1 drugs stir change in the beverage industry. Prominent UK fund manager Terry Smith publicly sold his Diageo stake, writing that he suspected "the entire drinks sector is in the early stages of being impacted negatively by weight-loss drugs." People taking GLP-1 drugs reported greater reductions in alcohol consumption than comparison groups, with the effect much stronger among individuals with diagnosed alcohol use disorder or hazardous drinking patterns.

### 3.2 Management Response

Diageo's "Accelerate" programme is progressing and is central to creating a more agile operating model. In November 2024, Diageo created the Diageo Luxury Group, a new global division which unites Diageo's luxury spirits and experiences for the first time, responsible for chartering luxury strategy and accelerating the growth of brands that retail at $100 and above, in partnership with their respective brand stewards globally.

## 4. EXTERNAL ANALYSIS: PESTEL

### Political
Diageo has anticipated and planned for a number of potential scenarios regarding tariffs, and the confirmation of the implementation of tariffs in the US posed a direct cost challenge to its import-dependent portfolio. As of 2024, Diageo also faces significant regulatory challenges following Brexit, with increased customs documentation requirements and potential tariff impacts.

### Economic
While consumers continue to be cautious, Diageo is focused on strengthening the resilience of its business through operational excellence, productivity and strategic investments to win quality market share. ~30% of 2024 net sales came from Africa and Asia, so macro slowdowns pose material risk, but middle-class growth is driving larger branded-spirit demand. FX swings, higher input/energy costs and rising rates compressed margins in FY24 — GBP strength could cut reported revenue materially; Diageo used hedges and held £9.8bn net cash with net debt/EBITDA ~1.1x.

### Social
From January to July 2024, IWSR data found that alcohol-free 'spirits' volumes in the US were up by 29% compared to the same period in 2023. This is driven by a generational shift — particularly among Millennials and Gen Z — toward moderation and wellness. The rise of GLP-1 might also see the emergence of non-alcoholic drinks that offer not just taste and ritual, but also nutritional value.

### Technological
Diageo has invested significantly in e-commerce capabilities. As CFO, Lavanya drove several large transformation projects including the change of functional currency from GBP to USD, instituting a new Global Process Ownership model, and the implementation of SAP 4/HANA. In Asia Pacific, Diageo deployed an award-winning AI-led consumer campaign, demonstrating its growing digital marketing capabilities.

### Environmental

Non-alcoholic and RTD expansion saw Guinness 0.0 and Tanqueray 0.0 expanded in 2025 after 15% YoY demand growth for premium alcohol-free alternatives, while R&D prioritised decarbonisation, with the first carbon-neutral distillery in North America.

### Legal
Stricter rules on alcohol advertising to under-25s, emerging EU health labelling requirements, and minimum unit pricing legislation in several markets are increasing compliance costs and limiting promotional reach — all creating structural headwinds for volume-based growth strategies.

## 5. INDUSTRY ANALYSIS: PORTER'S FIVE FORCES

### Force 1: Competitive Rivalry — **HIGH**
Diageo operates in a highly competitive global alcoholic beverage market. Pernod Ricard, a French company, is Diageo's most direct competitor and the second-largest spirits company globally. Pernod Ricard doubled down on tequila and premiumised core whiskies in 2024–2025, targeting the fastest-growing profit pools. Other major rivals include Beam Suntory, Bacardi, Brown-Forman (Jack Daniel's), and LVMH (Moët Hennessy). Competition occurs across brand equity, distribution depth, marketing spend, and innovation — all in a market experiencing volume stagnation in mature geographies.

### Force 2: Threat of New Entrants — **LOW TO MEDIUM**
Diageo can take advantage of the economies of scale it has within the industry, fighting off new entrants through its cost advantage. It can focus on innovation to differentiate its products from new entrants and spend on marketing to build strong brand identification. However, the no/low-alcohol segment presents lower barriers, enabling new entrants (e.g., Seedlip, Lyre's) to disrupt without the constraints of alcohol production.

### Force 3: Bargaining Power of Suppliers — **LOW TO MEDIUM**
The number of suppliers in the industry in which Diageo operates is large compared to buyers, limiting individual supplier power. However, agave is a geographically concentrated crop with a 7–10 year growth cycle, creating supply chain vulnerability — as the Casamigos supply shortage demonstrated.

### Force 4: Bargaining Power of Buyers — **MEDIUM TO HIGH**
Walmart, Costco, and Tesco control 59.2% of global alcohol retail distribution channels. These retailers negotiate prices aggressively, demanding volume discounts. Diageo's iconic brands create consumer pull that limits substitution risk, and investment in DTC and e-commerce is partially mitigating this power.

### Force 5: Threat of Substitutes — **MEDIUM TO HIGH (and Rising)**
This is the most structurally significant force. The global alcoholic beverage industry is undergoing significant shifts, with consumers increasingly prioritising quality over quantity, a trend known as selective premiumisation. Despite economic pressures, premium and above spirits and wine volumes saw a 3% decline in the first half of 2024. The no/low-alcohol category, cannabis-infused beverages, and GLP-1 drugs all represent growing substitution threats.

## 6. INTERNAL ANALYSIS

### 6.1 SWOT Analysis

**Strengths**
1. **Unrivalled Brand Portfolio:** Diageo's extensive portfolio of well-established brands, such as Johnnie Walker, Smirnoff, and Guinness, contributes to their competitive advantage in the market. Their intellectual property, including trademarks and patents, protect their brands and innovations.
2. **Scale & Premiumisation Capability:** Diageo is the world's largest spirits producer by revenue, holding roughly 6% of the total beverage alcohol market value and leading premium spirits categories globally.
3. **Financial Resilience:** Strong free cash flow generation and net debt/EBITDA of ~1.1x provide firepower for investment and M&A.
4. **Innovation:** The Distill Ventures accelerator incubates craft and emerging brands, providing an early-warning system for market disruption.

**Weaknesses**
1. **North American Over-Dependence:** ~40% of revenues concentrated in a single geography creates dangerous earnings volatility.
2. **Route-to-Market Vulnerability:** The US three-tier distribution system creates an intermediary layer that distorts demand signals.
3. **Portfolio Complexity:** Managing 200+ brands across 180 markets creates resource dilution and strategic focus challenges.
4. **Slow No/Low Response:** While Diageo already has non-alcoholic versions of its Gordon's, Gordon's Pink and Tanqueray gins, as well as Captain Morgan rum, its response has been relatively slow compared to the pace of consumer demand growth.

**Opportunities**
1. **Emerging Markets:** Diageo's efforts in market expansion, especially in emerging economies, are vital for its future prospects, capitalising on growing consumer bases and increasing disposable incomes.
2. **No/Low Alcohol:** The 29% YoY growth in US alcohol-free spirits volumes represents a major white space opportunity.
3. **Luxury Tier:** The $100+ price tier is a fast-growing price tier in international spirits since 2020.
4. **Guinness Momentum:** Don Julio, Guinness and Crown Royal Blackberry are standout performers, with Guinness growing as the fastest-growing imported beer in the on-trade.

**Threats**
1. **GLP-1 Drugs:** Structural reduction in alcohol consumption driven by widespread adoption of weight-loss drugs.
2. **US Tariffs:** Tariffs on Scotch whisky and other imported spirits could significantly increase costs in Diageo's most profitable market.
3. **Intensifying Competition:** Despite a strong overall market position, some of Diageo's leading spirit brands experienced notable declines in the first half of fiscal year 2025, indicating an intensely competitive environment where consumer preferences are constantly shifting.
4. **Climate Change:** Climate-driven yield variability affecting agave and barley prices poses a material risk to production costs.

## 10. THE CORE STRATEGIC PROBLEM

Synthesising the above analyses, Diageo's challenge can be distilled into a **single core tension:**

> *Diageo is a company built for a world of rising alcohol consumption, but must now build a strategy for a world of moderating or declining alcohol consumption — while simultaneously defending its premium positioning against intensifying competition and cyclical demand disruption.*

This manifests in four specific strategic problems:
1. **Geographic concentration risk:** Over-dependence on North America in a market experiencing both cyclical and structural headwinds.
2. **Portfolio relevance risk:** Core brands losing relevance in maturing categories while no/low grows without Diageo's full participation.
3. **Distribution model risk:** The US three-tier system creates dangerous information asymmetry and inventory volatility.
4. **Structural demand risk:** GLP-1 drugs and the sober-curious movement represent potential long-run demand destruction.

## 12. CONCLUSION

Diageo's strategic situation in 2024–2025 is one of the most complex in its 27-year history as a combined entity. The company faces a rare convergence of cyclical headwinds (North American inventory correction, LAC consumer weakness), structural disruptions (sober-curious movement, GLP-1 drugs, craft competition), and operational failures (Casamigos supply, LAC inventory management) — all occurring simultaneously.

Yet the fundamental strategic assets remain exceptional. Diageo's strong brand portfolio, global presence, and extensive distribution network — anchored by the irreplaceable heritage of Johnnie Walker, Guinness, and Don Julio — provide a platform that no competitor can easily replicate, as confirmed by the VRIO analysis.

The strategic imperative is clear: **Diageo must evolve from a company that grows by selling more alcohol to more people, into a company that grows by selling better experiences to a more discerning, more moderate global consumer.** This means:

1. **Fixing the operational basics** (US distribution, Casamigos supply) to restore near-term performance.
2. **Pivoting the portfolio** toward no/low-alcohol, super-premium luxury, and emerging markets — the three segments most resilient to structural demand disruption.
3. **Future-proofing the business model** by understanding and responding to the GLP-1 phenomenon before it becomes an existential threat.

Diageo recognises the need to drive meaningful growth opportunities in an evolving TBA landscape and is sharpening its strategy to accelerate growth. The Diageo Luxury Group launch and the Accelerate programme suggest that leadership is moving in the right direction. The question is whether they are moving fast enough. In a world where the rules of the beverage alcohol industry are being rewritten, speed of strategic adaptation is itself a competitive advantage.
