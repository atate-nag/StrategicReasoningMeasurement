# SLB (Schlumberger): Strategic Case Study
## *Navigating the Dual Mandate — Sustaining Core Oilfield Dominance While Pivoting to an Energy Technology Future*

---

## 📌 The Strategic Situation

SLB's strategy is focused on three engines of growth: **Core** (traditional oilfield services), **Digital** (cloud, AI, and edge platforms), and **New Energy** (carbon capture, geothermal, hydrogen). The central strategic tension is a classic **exploration vs. exploitation** dilemma — SLB must exploit its dominant position in oil and gas while exploring nascent energy technology markets. Too slow on transition and SLB becomes a stranded asset; too fast and it cannibalises the core business that funds the pivot.

SLB's full-year 2024 revenue was $36.29 billion, a 10% increase year-on-year. Full-year EPS excluding charges and credits was $3.41, a 14% increase year-on-year.

---

## 🌍 PESTEL Analysis

**Political**: With ~70% of 2025 revenue from international markets, SLB is highly exposed to regional conflicts and trade sanctions; ongoing Middle East and Eastern Europe tensions threaten personnel safety and asset integrity.

**Economic**: The industry is navigating global economic uncertainty stemming from supply-demand imbalance and recent tariff announcements. In this environment, commodity prices are challenged, and until they stabilise, customers are likely to take a more cautious approach to near-term activity and discretionary spending.

**Technological**: The company plans to invest $1.5 billion in digital technology for 2024. A primary focus is the integration of AI and machine learning into energy services.

**Environmental**: SLB's New Energy strategy continues to expand exposure beyond oil and gas with significant growth momentum in low-carbon markets, focusing on industrial decarbonisation (carbon capture and sequestration, low-carbon hydrogen), renewables and energy efficiency (geothermal, energy storage), and critical minerals such as lithium from brine.

## 💡 Five Strategic Recommendations

### 1. Execute a "Digital First" Commercial Strategy *(0–18 months)*
Move Delfi™ and Lumi™ from subscription pricing to **outcome-based contracts** — charging a percentage of production gains enabled by AI. The Digital & Integration segment is a key growth driver, with digital revenue increasing by 17% year-on-year in Q1 2025. Total digital revenue reached $2.44 billion in 2024, a 20% increase from the previous year. Target $3.5 billion by end-2025.

### 2. Disciplined ChampionX Integration *(0–24 months)*
The acquisition will expand SLB's presence in the less cyclical and growing production and recovery space that is closely aligned with SLB's returns-focused, capital-light strategy. Establish an Integration Management Office, cross-sell to top 50 drilling clients, and capture $400M+ in synergies.

### 3. Scale New Energy Through Milestone-Gated Investment *(18 months–5 years)*
The New Energy vertical is projected to reach a $1 billion run-rate by end-2025 across carbon capture, hydrogen, geothermal, and storage. Implement a "scale or exit" portfolio review annually to prevent capital being trapped in sub-scale ventures. Target $3 billion by 2028.

### 4. Strengthen Geopolitical Resilience *(Ongoing)*
Reduce Middle East revenue concentration below 30% of total revenue. Implement quarterly geopolitical scenario planning and expand political risk insurance coverage across high-risk jurisdictions.

### 5. Protect the Digital Moat Against Big Tech *(Ongoing)*
Rather than competing with AWS/Microsoft/Google, deepen partnerships to make SLB the **domain-expert layer** on top of cloud infrastructure. Build a proprietary anonymised subsurface dataset as a data moat. SLB's collaboration with Microsoft accelerates the development and deployment of industry-specific generative AI models across SLB's global platforms, including its Delfi™ digital platform and Lumi™ data and AI platform.

## 🎯 Conclusion

SLB's three-engine strategy is conceptually sound, but the decisive variable is **execution discipline**. Digital acceleration funds the New Energy build; ChampionX integration deepens the production lifecycle moat; geopolitical resilience protects the cash flows that fund everything else. If executed with precision, SLB will emerge from this decade not merely as the world's largest oilfield services company, but as the world's leading **energy technology company**.
