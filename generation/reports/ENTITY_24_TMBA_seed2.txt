# 🏎️ MERCEDES-BENZ GROUP AG: NAVIGATING THE CROSSROADS
## A Strategic Case Study | 2021–2025

---

## 1. EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

Mercedes-Benz Group AG, one of the world's most iconic luxury automotive brands, faces a defining strategic inflection point. After years of record profitability, the company's 2024 operating results showed a 31% decline to €13.6 billion EBIT, with passenger car segment margins falling from 12.6% to 8.1%. This was driven by a collapse in Chinese market demand, a stalling EV transition, and intensifying competition from both legacy rivals and disruptive new entrants.

This case study analyses the strategic situation using **PESTEL, Porter's Five Forces, SWOT, VRIO, Bowman's Strategy Clock**, and the **Ansoff Matrix**, leading to a set of integrated strategic recommendations.

---

## 2. COMPANY BACKGROUND

Mercedes-Benz Group AG, headquartered in Stuttgart, Germany, traces its origins to 1886 when Karl Benz patented the world's first petrol-powered automobile. Today, it operates across two primary segments — **Mercedes-Benz Cars** and **Mercedes-Benz Vans** — selling approximately **1.98 million passenger cars** in 2024 across more than 190 countries.

| Revenue | €145.6bn | €152.4bn | **-4.5%** |
| EBIT | €13.6bn | €19.7bn | **-30.8%** |

| EPS | €10.19 | €13.46 | **-24.3%** |
| Cars Adjusted EBIT | €8.7bn | €14.3bn | **-39.2%** |
| Cars RoS Margin | 8.1% | 12.6% | **-4.5pp** |

Mercedes-Benz Group has reported revenue of €145.6bn for full year 2024, marking a 4.5% decrease from the €152.4bn recorded in 2023. The portfolio spans entry luxury (A-Class, CLA), core luxury (C-Class, E-Class, GLC), top-end luxury (S-Class, AMG), and ultra-luxury (Maybach, G-Class). In 2021, the company announced a bold **"luxury-first" pivot**, deliberately shedding volume to focus on higher-margin vehicles. This strategy delivered record profits in 2021–2022 but has since come under severe pressure.

## 3. THE STRATEGIC SITUATION

### 3.1 The China Crisis

In China, Mercedes's biggest single market, sales dropped seven percent in 2024. German manufacturers all invested heavily in China in recent decades but are facing fierce competition from local rivals. The 3% decline to 1.98 million vehicles was led by a slump in deliveries in China, where consumers are shunning models like the S-Class and Maybach in favour of local brands. Sales in the country dropped 7% to 683,600 cars from a year earlier.

China — once a growth engine — saw a 27% sales decline in some periods, driven by fierce competition from local EV makers like BYD and Xiaomi. These dual pressures have forced Mercedes to accelerate U.S. production strategies. Mercedes-Benz has announced plans to cut 15% of its workforce in China as the luxury automaker grapples with intense competition and a slowing Chinese market.

### 3.2 The EV Transition Dilemma

In July 2021, CEO Ola Källenius declared Mercedes-Benz would go "all-in on electric," targeting an all-electric portfolio by 2030. Mercedes-Benz Group AG is backing away from ambitious electric-vehicle goals as weakening sales push the luxury automaker to its tried-and-true combustion engines for the foreseeable future. Sales of Mercedes' fully electric cars plunged by nearly a quarter last year, as consumer demand waned.

By 2024, the company was pushing back sales targets for electrified vehicles; targeting 50 percent electrified sales by 2030, rather than 2025.

### 3.3 Profitability Under Siege

Mercedes-Benz Group AG plans to step up cost reductions after fierce competition and weaker demand in China hit the luxury-car maker's profits. The company's key gauge of profitability slid to 4.7% in the third quarter, undershooting its minimum target of 8% and the lowest level since the carmaker split from its truck business in late 2021.

In response, Mercedes-Benz has launched the "Next Level Performance" plan, aiming to reduce production costs by 10% by 2027 and permanently save €5 billion, with €2.5 billion expected to be saved in 2025. This global cost-cutting initiative will affect all business areas, with some departments expected to see budget reductions of up to 30%. Reports suggest this action could involve up to 20,000 job cuts worldwide.

### 3.4 The Software-Defined Vehicle Race

Mercedes-Benz's strategy of developing its own automotive OS aligns with the industry trend toward software-defined vehicles. By 2025, Mercedes-Benz intends to target €1 billion from software-enabled, features-on-demand services. The vertical integration of MB.OS will allow for a continuous, iterative approach to product development with an increased emphasis on software.

## 4. EXTERNAL ANALYSIS — PESTEL

### 🔴 Political
- The EU's 2035 ICE ban creates regulatory urgency for full electrification.
- A U.S.-EU trade deal finalized in July 2025 reduced tariffs on European car imports from 27.5% to 15%, offering partial relief but doing little to eliminate structural headwinds.
- China geopolitical tensions and trade restrictions create uncertainty for Mercedes-Benz's largest single market.

### 🟠 Economic
- The year 2024 has been a difficult year for Mercedes-Benz. Between the slowdown in demand for electric vehicles, the stagnation of the Chinese market, the crisis in the luxury sector and the difficulties of the German industry as a whole, the car manufacturer is showing a sharp decline in annual results.
- Mercedes attributed the decline in sales of its top-of-the-range models to weaker demand for electric cars, model changes and market conditions in China. The crisis in the Chinese real estate market has put many wealthy customers off buying.
- Currency volatility — a strong Euro compresses margins on exports to key markets.

### 🟡 Social
- Sales to customers under 45 grew by 12% year-over-year in 2024. This younger demographic now accounts for nearly 28% of total sales volume, with a high affinity for technology and sustainability.
- Rising Chinese consumer nationalism is eroding the status premium of European luxury brands among younger domestic consumers.
- ESG-conscious buyers increasingly demand carbon-neutral manufacturing and sustainable supply chains.

### 🟢 Technological
- MB.OS provides chip-to-cloud integration, decoupling hardware from software to enable faster feature rollouts and new service monetisation.
- Mercedes-Benz leads auto tech, having rolled out Level 3 autonomous driving to 12 markets by end-2025 and deployed MB.OS across 1.2 million vehicles, boosting software revenue to €3.1 billion in 2025.
- AI-powered personalisation and in-vehicle digital experiences are becoming key purchase criteria.

### 🔵 Environmental
- In 2024, Mercedes-Benz cut carbon emissions by 75% (one year ahead of its 2030 target), with the Rastatt plant using 100% green energy for electric CLA production.
- Mercedes-Benz has committed to carbon neutrality across its value chain by 2039.

### ⚫ Legal
- GDPR and equivalent data privacy laws in China and the US constrain the use of connected vehicle data — a critical asset for software revenue models.
- Product liability and safety regulations for autonomous driving systems are becoming more stringent globally.

## 5. INDUSTRY ANALYSIS — PORTER'S FIVE FORCES (Porter, 1979)

### ⚔️ Force 1: Competitive Rivalry — **HIGH**

Competitors include BMW, Audi, Porsche, and Tesla across performance, EVs, and software; Mercedes emphasises brand cachet, dealer network, captive finance, and a 140-year heritage as differentiators. China competition and discounting intensified in 2024–2025; Tesla's price agility and strong local EV players pressure margins and share.

In 2024, the company faces pressure from established rivals like BMW and Audi, as well as new entrants like Tesla and various Chinese EV manufacturers. The competitive moat is narrowing, particularly in China and the premium EV segment.

**Assessment: HIGH — intensifying due to Chinese EV entrants and Tesla's pricing aggression.**

---

### 🚪 Force 2: Threat of New Entrants — **MEDIUM**

Traditional barriers (capital requirements, regulatory compliance, distribution networks) remain formidable in automotive manufacturing. However, the EV transition has lowered some of these barriers, as EV drivetrains are mechanically simpler and software-first companies have demonstrated new entry is possible. Tesla, with its disruptive electric vehicle technology, has emerged as a formidable competitor, particularly in the premium EV segment. This challenges Mercedes-Benz's traditional dominance.

**Assessment: MEDIUM — EV transition is reducing traditional barriers; state-backed Chinese entrants are a structural threat.**

---

### 🔩 Force 3: Bargaining Power of Suppliers — **MEDIUM-HIGH**

Mercedes-Benz depends on a complex global supply chain encompassing semiconductor manufacturers, battery cell suppliers (CATL, Samsung SDI), rare earth material producers, and specialised component makers. Material and fixed costs are being targeted through supplier collaboration, design optimisation, and the "local-for-local" manufacturing strategy, increasing from 60% to 70%. Battery cell supply remains concentrated among a small number of Asian suppliers, giving them significant leverage.

**Assessment: MEDIUM-HIGH — battery and semiconductor suppliers hold meaningful leverage in the EV transition.**

---

### 🛒 Force 4: Bargaining Power of Buyers — **MEDIUM-HIGH**

Customers in the premium and luxury automotive segment wield considerable bargaining power, largely due to the wide array of high-quality alternatives available from rivals such as BMW, Audi, and Lexus. The company has been forced to offer consumer incentives to combat slowing EV demand, particularly in China where luxury spending has been hit by economic slowdown.

**Assessment: MEDIUM-HIGH — increasing price transparency and competitive alternatives are strengthening buyer power.**

---

### 🔄 Force 5: Threat of Substitutes — **MEDIUM**

Urban transit, congestion pricing and 56% urbanisation in 2024 reduce private-car need, yet luxury demand (Mercedes ~2.0M deliveries) persists as status and convenience resist full substitution. High-quality used Mercedes vehicles deliver similar utility at lower cost, making them a credible substitute for new sales; certified pre-owned programmes add warranty and manufacturer financing that further bridge the gap.

**Assessment: MEDIUM — growing in urban markets; status and convenience factors provide some insulation.**

## 7. STRATEGIC POSITION — BOWMAN'S STRATEGY CLOCK (Bowman & Faulkner, 1996)

Bowman's Strategy Clock maps competitive positions on two axes: **Perceived Added Value** and **Price**.

```
HIGH
VALUE  │    [5] Focused      [6] Risky High
       │    Differentiation  Margins
       │    ← INTENDED  ←←← HISTORIC
       │
       │    [4] Differentiation
       │    ← ACTUAL DRIFT
       │
       │    [3] Hybrid
       │    ← TESLA / CHINESE EV THREAT
       │
LOW    └────────────────────────────────→
            LOW PRICE            HIGH PRICE
```

Mercedes-Benz has historically occupied **Position 5: Focused Differentiation** — high perceived value at a premium price. The 2021 "luxury-first" strategy was an attempt to push toward **Position 6: Risky High Margins**.

**Current Strategic Tension:**
- The **intended position** remains focused differentiation (Position 5), reinforced by the Mythos ultra-luxury push.
- The **actual drift** is toward **Position 4** — where the company is being forced to offer discounts and incentives to maintain volume, diluting the premium positioning.
- The **competitive threat** from Tesla and BYD occupies **Position 3: Hybrid** — offering high perceived value (technology, software, sustainability) at moderate prices, attracting younger affluent consumers.

> **Strategic Implication:** Mercedes-Benz must urgently **reinforce its Position 5 differentiation** through genuine software and technology leadership. The Mythos series anchors the top of the position, but the core portfolio (C-Class, E-Class) is most vulnerable to the hybrid threat.

## 8. GROWTH OPTIONS — ANSOFF MATRIX (Ansoff, 1957)

| | **Existing Products** | **New Products** |
|---|---|---|
| **Existing Markets** | **Market Penetration** ⬅️ Lowest Risk | **Product Development** |
| **New Markets** | **Market Development** | **Diversification** ➡️ Highest Risk |

### Quadrant 1 — Market Penetration (Lowest Risk)
Recapture share in core European and North American markets through refreshed model launches. Mercedes-Benz's product launch programme starts in 2025 with the CLA, followed by a major upgrade of the S-Class in 2026, an all-electric GLC and C-Class, as well as a string of BEV and electrified high-tech ICE launches at Mercedes-AMG. Overall, sales are expected to gain traction after dozens of new or refreshed models reach markets until 2027.

### Quadrant 2 — Product Development (Medium Risk)
The most critical growth opportunity lies in **software-defined products and services**. The use of digital tools also allows Mercedes-Benz to achieve greater levels of efficiency, and the MB.OS lays the foundation for all domains, especially infotainment and automated driving functionalities. The **Mythos ultra-luxury series** and the **electric CLA** (on the new MMA platform) are the two flagship product development initiatives.

### Quadrant 3 — Market Development (Medium Risk)
Geographic diversification away from China-dependence is strategically urgent. India, Southeast Asia, and the Middle East represent high-growth markets for luxury vehicles. Mercedes-Benz is increasing its "local-for-local" manufacturing from 60% to 70% of sales in key markets, supporting geographic expansion while reducing tariff exposure.

### Quadrant 4 — Diversification (Highest Risk)
Mercedes-Benz's Level 3 autonomous driving capability positions it to participate in emerging **mobility-as-a-service (MaaS)** and robotaxi markets. This should be pursued cautiously through **strategic partnerships** rather than full vertical integration, given the significant capital requirements and operational complexity.

## 9. STRATEGIC RECOMMENDATIONS & ACTION PLAN

The following six integrated recommendations are proposed for Mercedes-Benz Group AG for the period **2025–2030**.

---

### 🔧 Recommendation 1: Accelerate MB.OS Deployment — Build a Software Revenue Engine

**Strategic Rationale:** The VRIO analysis identifies MB.OS as a *potential* sustained competitive advantage — but only if fully deployed. The PESTEL analysis confirms the shift to software-defined vehicles as the most significant technological trend reshaping the industry.

**Actions:**
- Prioritise MB.OS rollout across the entire model range by 2027, starting with the electric CLA (2025) and S-Class refresh (2026).
- Launch a tiered **features-on-demand subscription model** (enhanced autonomous driving, premium audio, advanced navigation) to generate recurring software revenue.
- Leverage the NVIDIA Omniverse partnership to reduce coordination processes by 50% and double the speed of production conversion using digital twin technology.
- Establish a dedicated **Mercedes Digital Services division** with P&L accountability.

**KPIs:** Software revenue >€2 billion by 2027; MB.OS deployed in >80% of new vehicle sales by end-2026.

---

### 🇨🇳 Recommendation 2: Execute a China Recovery Strategy — "Premium Resilience"

**Strategic Rationale:** China's sales decline represents the single largest financial threat to Mercedes-Benz. The Porter's Five Forces analysis reveals intensifying competitive rivalry from local EV makers. However, complete withdrawal would be strategically catastrophic.

**Actions:**
- **Localise the EV product line**: Develop China-specific EV variants through the Beijing Benz joint venture that integrate local digital ecosystems (WeChat, Baidu Maps, Alipay).
- **Protect the ultra-luxury segment**: Concentrate Maybach and G-Class marketing investment — these remain aspirational status symbols even as mainstream consumers shift to domestic brands.
- Pursue a "local-for-local" strategy — such as producing long-wheelbase GLC variants in Beijing — to ensure alignment with regional demand.
- Reduce China revenue dependency from ~35% to ~25% of global sales by 2030 through geographic diversification.

**KPIs:** China RoS margin recovery to >8% by 2026; China revenue share reduced to <28% by 2030.

---

### ⚡ Recommendation 3: Rebalance the EV Strategy — "Electrification with Pragmatism"

**Strategic Rationale:** Regulatory pressure makes full electrification inevitable in the long run, but the current market reality demands a pragmatic, phased approach. The Ansoff Matrix identifies the electric CLA and GLC as critical product development priorities.

**Actions:**
- **Commit to the MMA platform** as the foundation for next-generation core luxury EVs. The electric CLA must be positioned as a genuine Tesla Model 3 competitor in software experience, range, and value.
- Maintain ICE production for 2025–2027 in lagging markets while expanding EV sales.
- Secure long-term battery supply agreements with CATL and Samsung SDI while co-developing next-generation solid-state battery technology to achieve range parity with Chinese competitors by 2028.
- Develop a compelling **EV charging ecosystem** through partnerships to remove range anxiety as a purchase barrier.

**KPIs:** Electric CLA achieves 200,000+ annual sales by 2027; EV sales mix >30% by 2027; battery cost per kWh reduced by 20% by 2027.

---

### 🌍 Recommendation 4: Geographic Diversification — "Beyond China"

**Strategic Rationale:** The SWOT analysis identifies China over-dependence as a critical weakness. The Ansoff Matrix's market development quadrant points to India, Southeast Asia, and the Middle East as high-priority growth markets.

**Actions:**
- **India**: Establish or expand local manufacturing to avoid import tariffs and capture India's rapidly growing premium segment. Target 100,000 annual sales by 2030.
- **Middle East**: Expand the Maybach and AMG product presence in GCC markets, where oil wealth, low taxation, and a strong culture of automotive status symbols create ideal conditions for ultra-luxury sales.
- **Southeast Asia**: Develop right-hand-drive EV variants for Thailand, Indonesia, and Malaysia, where EV adoption is accelerating and government incentives are available.

**KPIs:** Non-China Asia sales growth >15% annually 2025–2028; China revenue share reduced to <28% by 2028.

---

### 👑 Recommendation 5: Protect Brand Heritage Through the "Mythos" Ultra-Luxury Strategy

**Strategic Rationale:** The VRIO analysis confirms that brand heritage is Mercedes-Benz's *only* sustained competitive advantage — one that Tesla and BYD cannot replicate. Bowman's Strategy Clock recommends anchoring the top of the differentiation position.

**Actions:**
- **Invest in the Mythos ultra-luxury series**: The AMG PureSpeed should be the first of at least 3–4 Mythos models by 2028, targeting ultra-high-net-worth individuals with deliberately limited production volumes to maintain exclusivity.
- **Protect the Maybach brand**: Resist any temptation to discount or broaden the Maybach range. Consider dedicated Maybach flagship experience centres in Dubai, Shanghai, New York, and London.
- **Enforce pricing discipline**: Avoid broad discounting that undermines brand equity. Use targeted, confidential incentives for fleet/lease customers rather than public price reductions.
- Reinforce the heritage narrative — Mercedes is selling heritage and luxury, and it is hard to put a discount sticker on that.

**KPIs:** Mythos/Maybach segment revenue >€5 billion annually by 2028; brand value recovery to >€60 billion by 2027.

---

### 💰 Recommendation 6: Execute "Next Level Performance" with Precision — Cost Without Compromise

**Strategic Rationale:** The financial analysis reveals an unsustainable cost structure at current revenue levels. The "Next Level Performance" plan is necessary but must be executed carefully to avoid quality deterioration or brand damage.

**Actions:**
- Reduce total production capacity from 2.5 million units to 2.0–2.2 million units, with reductions achieved through production rationalisation at the Beijing Benz joint venture and European plants.
- Protect R&D spending (~€14 billion annually) as the engine of future competitive advantage — cuts must come from manufacturing overhead, not innovation.
- Reduce production costs by 10% between 2024 and 2027 through digital tools and automation.
- Manage workforce reductions humanely through voluntary redundancy, early retirement, and retraining programmes to minimise industrial relations damage.

**KPIs:** €2.5 billion savings achieved in 2025; production cost per unit reduced by 10% by 2027; Cars RoS margin restored to >10% by 2027.

## 10. CONCLUSION

Mercedes-Benz Group AG stands at a genuine strategic crossroads. In response to its challenges, Mercedes has outlined a performance enhancement programme that includes major cost-cutting and a focus on combining innovative technology, strategic cost management, and a unified design approach across its vehicle lineup to strengthen its market position.

The analysis presented in this case study reveals a company with formidable enduring strengths — most notably its brand heritage, engineering excellence, and the emerging MB.OS platform — but also critical vulnerabilities in software capability, geographic concentration, and EV technology. The application of PESTEL, Porter's Five Forces, SWOT, VRIO, Bowman's Strategy Clock, and the Ansoff Matrix consistently points to the same strategic imperative:

> **Mercedes-Benz must leverage its irreplaceable brand heritage to fund and accelerate a genuine digital and electrification transformation, while pragmatically managing the transition timeline to preserve profitability.**

The six recommendations proposed — MB.OS acceleration, China recovery, EV rebalancing, geographic diversification, ultra-luxury brand protection, and disciplined cost management — are not independent initiatives but an **integrated strategic programme**. To strengthen its competitiveness and resilience, Mercedes-Benz has launched a comprehensive performance enhancement plan. The company readies its biggest-ever product and tech launch campaign with dozens of new or refreshed models until 2027.

Their collective execution represents Mercedes-Benz's most credible path to restoring its target double-digit RoS margin and securing its position as the world's pre-eminent luxury automotive brand in the electric, software-defined era.

*The stars of Mercedes-Benz's past were built on the internal combustion engine. Its stars of the future will be written in software.*
