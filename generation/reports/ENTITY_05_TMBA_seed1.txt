## 3. THE STRATEGIC SITUATION: A MULTI-DIMENSIONAL CRISIS

### 3.1 The Monsanto Acquisition: A Strategic Blunder

In June 2018, Bayer completed the acquisition of Monsanto — the largest acquisition in German corporate history. The strategic rationale was to combine Bayer's world-leading crop protection chemicals with Monsanto's dominant position in seeds and genetic traits, creating an integrated "one-stop shop" for farmers from seed to harvest.

However, Bayer fatally underestimated the scale of Monsanto's legal liabilities. Bayer appears to have underestimated the scale and durability of Monsanto's litigation risk. Within months of the deal closing, a California jury awarded $289 million to a former school groundskeeper, opening the floodgates to tens of thousands of additional lawsuits alleging that Roundup caused Non-Hodgkin's Lymphoma.

**The Litigation Toll:**
- As of October 2024, approximately 177,000 total claims had been filed, of which approximately 114,000 had been settled or were not eligible for various reasons.
- As of December 31, 2025, Bayer's provisions and liabilities for the glyphosate litigation totalled $11.3 billion (€9.6 billion).
- Bayer earned $2.9 billion from glyphosate sales last year, but payouts for Roundup litigation have put the company in the red for the past three years.

### 3.2 The Pharmaceutical Patent Cliff

Bayer's pharmaceutical division faces a significant "patent cliff" — the loss of exclusivity on its two largest products:
- By 2025, Bayer's pharmaceutical division expected Xarelto's EBITDA contribution to drop from €4.7 billion in 2023 to €3.5 billion — a staggering 25% erosion.
- Bayer lost its US patent on Xarelto, its factor Xa inhibitor, in 2025, which alone accounted for an estimated €1bn–€1.5bn of lost 2025 revenues. The company is set to lose its patent on Eylea in 2027.

### 3.3 Organisational Dysfunction

Anderson said that the 12 layers of management between himself and customers were "simply too much" and added that, by the end of 2024, the company would remove multiple layers of management and coordination.

### 3.4 Financial Distress

Net financial debt as of June 30, 2023, was 9.8 percent higher than at the end of March 2023, reaching €39.620 billion. Combined with ongoing litigation provisions, this severely constrained Bayer's strategic flexibility.

### 4.2 PORTER'S FIVE FORCES

#### Force 1: Competitive Rivalry — **HIGH** 🔴
From 2018 to 2020, the top four seed and agrochemical firms — Bayer, Syngenta Group, Corteva, and BASF — controlled 60–70% of the global pesticides market and 50–60% of the $45 billion global seed market. In pharmaceuticals, Bayer competes with Pfizer, Roche, Novartis, and AstraZeneca. Eylea competes with Regeneron's products directly in the ophthalmology market.

**Implication**: Bayer must continuously innovate and differentiate to defend its market position across all three divisions.

#### Force 2: Threat of New Entrants — **LOW to MODERATE** 🟡
- **Pharmaceuticals**: High barriers — massive R&D costs, lengthy regulatory approval, patent protection. However, Chinese generics manufacturers present a growing challenge.
- **Crop Science**: High capital requirements and regulatory hurdles. However, biological crop protection is a lower-barrier segment attracting new entrants.
- High entry barriers due to costs associated with research and development of new drugs, economies of scale in manufacturing, R&D, marketing, sales, and distribution protect incumbents like Bayer.

#### Force 3: Threat of Substitutes — **MODERATE to HIGH** 🟠
- **Pharmaceuticals**: Generic drugs post-patent are a direct substitute — acutely relevant given Xarelto/Eylea patent expiries. Biosimilars threaten Eylea specifically.
- **Crop Science**: Biological pesticides, organic farming methods, and precision agriculture represent growing substitutes for conventional crop protection products.
- **Consumer Health**: Private-label OTC products and natural remedies substitute for branded products like Aspirin and Claritin.

#### Force 4: Bargaining Power of Buyers — **MODERATE to HIGH** 🟠
- Hospital systems, pharmacy benefit managers (PBMs), and national health systems have significant purchasing power in pharmaceuticals.
- Without strong differentiation, Bayer may face pressure to lower prices due to informed customer choices, eroding its pricing power.
- Large agribusiness corporations and farming cooperatives have growing purchasing power in Crop Science.

#### Force 5: Bargaining Power of Suppliers — **LOW to MODERATE** 🟢
- Raw chemical inputs have multiple global suppliers, reducing supplier power in Crop Science.
- Dependence on Chinese and Indian API manufacturers creates some supply chain risk in Pharmaceuticals.
- Specialised biotechnology inputs (e.g., gene editing tools, microbial strains) may have more concentrated supply.

**Five Forces Summary**: The most intense competitive pressures facing Bayer are **rivalry** (multi-front competition), **substitutes** (generics, biologicals, biosimilars), and **buyer power** (healthcare systems, large agribusiness). These forces reinforce the imperative for continuous innovation and brand differentiation.

The company's simultaneous exposure to a pharmaceutical patent cliff compounded the financial vulnerability. ### 🔴 Root Cause 3: Organisational Sclerosis
Bayer's 12-layer management hierarchy and deeply bureaucratic culture created an organisation unable to respond quickly to changing competitive and regulatory conditions. Anderson described the 12 layers of management between himself and customers as "simply too much," necessitating a radical realignment of the company's internal culture. This organisational dysfunction is as much a cause of the crisis as the acquisition itself.

## 8. CONCLUSION

Bayer AG's strategic crisis is among the most complex and multi-dimensional in recent corporate history. The Bayer-Monsanto deal serves as a cautionary tale for corporate dealmakers, with pervasive impacts on agricultural practices and policies at the European and global level. The $63 billion Monsanto acquisition — intended as a bold strategic move to create an agricultural powerhouse — has instead become a masterclass in what happens when strategic ambition outstrips due diligence, financial prudence, and risk management.

However, Bayer is not beyond recovery. Bayer's strength lies in its diverse portfolio across pharmaceuticals, consumer health, and crop science, which cushions against market-specific risks.
